03
Email / DM stage metrics
The blind spot in most funnels. META and Stripe handle the top and bottom automatically — this middle section is invisible unless you log it.
The gap nobody talks about: Everything between form submit and payment is untracked unless you build it into your Sheet. There are no automated tools here. It's all in how you structure your status column and how disciplined you are about updating it.
Key insight — once you have 20-30 leads
$15 CPL
5% lead-to-sale
Cheap leads, bad buyers. META is winning on volume, not quality. You're paying for browsers.
vs
$25 CPL
20% lead-to-sale
More expensive leads, actual buyers. This audience converts. You want more of these, not fewer.
META optimises for the cheapest leads, not the best ones. Your Sheet is the only thing that tells you which ad creative or audience is actually producing buyers — not just form submissions. That's the insight that justifies shifting budget.
The four metrics that matter in this stage
💬
Reply rate
% of submissions who reply to first follow-up
Target: 30-40%+. Below that means your follow-up email is weak, the form is attracting low-intent leads, or you're waiting too long. Respond within the hour while they're still warm — speed is the biggest lever.
Track in Sheet: count rows where status moved from contacted to replied
Set a personal rule: follow up within 60 minutes of receiving a form submission in Gmail
🔗
Payment link open rate
Sent but no response is its own drop-off stage
Stripe's built-in payment link has no open tracking. "Sent payment, no reply" is a distinct drop-off point — different problem from "never replied at all." Track these separately or they both look like the same dead lead.
Use payment_sent as a distinct status — separate from replied and dead
Add a notes column — use it to flag "sent Stripe link, no response" so you can follow up once more
💰
Lead-to-sale conversion rate
The main number. Converted / total submissions.
For cold traffic at $97, 10-20% is strong. Below 10% usually means the landing page is attracting the wrong people, or the follow-up isn't closing. This is the number that tells you whether you have a traffic problem or a conversion problem.
Formula: =COUNTIF(status,"converted")/COUNTA(date) — no complex formulas needed
Check this by source — =COUNTIFS(status,"converted",source,"meta") vs organic/direct
⏱
Time-to-close
Days between form submit and payment
If most sales happen within 24 hours and the rest never convert, stop chasing after day 3. This isn't a nurture-over-weeks product. Redirect that energy to new traffic instead of burning time on cold leads.
Add a converted_date column — calculate days to close with =converted_date - date
After 20 sales, mark any lead dead if no payment after 3 days — stick to this rule
The six status values your Sheet needs
StatusMeaning
new Form received, not yet followed up
contacted First follow-up sent
replied They responded
payment_sent Stripe link sent
converted Paid — $97 received
dead No reply after 3 days, or explicitly declined
One extra column worth adding: source, populated from the UTM parameter when the lead came in. Once you have 30+ leads you'll start seeing whether DM leads convert better than ad leads, or whether a specific campaign attracts buyers vs browsers. That's the insight that lets you shift budget — and it costs nothing to track from day one.

04
META Pixel events
Three events to fire. Each closes a different loop in your attribution chain.
Why this matters: Without Pixel events, META optimises for link clicks. With them, you can optimise for Lead or Purchase events — dramatically better ad delivery and lower CPL over time.
📄
PageView
Fires on landing page load
Add base Pixel code to <head> of landing page
Replace PIXEL_ID placeholder with your actual META Pixel ID
Verify with META Pixel Helper Chrome extension — green tick on load
📋
Lead event
Fires on Web3Forms submit
Add fbq('track','Lead') to your form submit callback (after Web3Forms success)
Submit a test form — confirm Lead event fires in Pixel Helper
Check Events Manager in META Business Suite — Lead event visible within 24hrs
💳
Purchase event
Fires on Stripe redirect
Create a thank-you page hosted on GitHub Pages (e.g. /thankyou.html)
Add fbq('track','Purchase',{value:97,currency:'USD'}) to that page's <head>
In Stripe: Payment links → After payment → set redirect URL to your thank-you page
Do a test purchase — confirm Purchase event fires and appears in Events Manager
