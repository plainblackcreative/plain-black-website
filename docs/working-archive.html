Decision Rationale

Why it was held, not scrapped

The decision wasn't that the feature lacks value. It's that the value doesn't justify ongoing cost at a one-off price point yet.

Case For Including
  • Reduces drop-off at highest-risk steps (Pixel install, Make.com)
  • Upsell triage fires at the moment of friction, not days later
  • API cost is ~$0.10 per session with a message cap, negligible at $99
  • One upsell conversion per 50 clients covers all API costs for the cohort
  • Completion rate improvement directly affects refund rate and word-of-mouth
Case Against (Current Stage)
  • Any ongoing cost after a one-off sale creates model risk at low volume
  • Product philosophy is zero post-sale involvement, this complicates that
  • Bad AI response at a critical moment damages trust in the whole product
  • Adds a component that can break, update, and require maintenance
  • Simple products convert better at this price point

The Core Idea

Price anchor, then relief

What This Is

Run a META ad for a Done For You agency-style service at a price that makes people flinch ($1,500 to $3,000+). Let it build a retarget pool of people who saw the price and didn't buy. Then immediately retarget that audience with the $99 DIY playbook, using headlines that reference the agency price directly.

The DFY offer does not need to be a real product you fulfill. It is a price anchor and an audience filter, running at the same time.

Why It Works

Cold audiences don't know what agencies charge. The DFY ad puts an exact figure in their head. When the retarget ad arrives and says "agencies charge $2,400 for this, here's the same outcome for $99" the contrast is concrete, not conceptual. This is one of the most reliable structures in direct response: show the expensive version, then offer the relief.

What the DFY Ad Does

Builds a warm retarget pool of high-intent buyers who are clearly thinking about marketing spend. Filters out people who aren't in buying mode at all.

What the Retarget Ad Does

Arrives with the price already anchored. Converts the same audience on the $99 offer using the DFY price as direct contrast in the headline.

The Ad Sequence

Four steps, one mechanic

1
DFY Agency Ad (Cold Audience)
Run a credible agency-positioned ad. Clean creative, professional tone. Price visible or implied to be $1,500 to $3,000+. CTA goes to a waitlist form or "get a custom quote" page. No fulfillment commitment required. Anyone who clicks and does not convert joins the retarget pool.
2
Retarget Pool Builds (48 to 72 Hours)
META pixel fires on everyone who landed on the DFY page. This audience has already self-identified as someone actively thinking about marketing. They saw a price. They chose not to act. They are warm and ready for contrast messaging.
3
DIY Playbook Retarget Ad
Hit the retarget pool with the $99 offer. Reference the exact dollar figure they just saw. Use contrast framing. This audience is primed for relief. The anchor does most of the selling.
4
Standard Funnel From Here
Retarget ad sends to the existing playbook landing page. No changes to the page needed. The anchor effect carries through from the ad creative itself.
Critical Requirement

The DFY ad and its landing page need to look genuinely real. If the agency offer reads as fake or staged, the whole anchor falls apart and the retarget loses credibility. It does not need to be a working product. It needs to look like one.

The Landing Page Question

Should the landing page include a DFY tab?

Decision: No, Not Yet

The anchor mechanic belongs in the ad sequence, not the landing page. The existing $99 landing page works as-is. The retarget ad does the contrast work before the visitor arrives. Adding a DFY pricing section is a future option if the sequence converts well.

DFY CTA goes to a waitlist or quote form, not a checkout. No fulfillment commitment. Test after the ad sequence has validated the concept.

Model Impact Assessment

What changes, what doesn't

No ChangeCore Model

No ongoing work. No fulfillment. No subscriptions. One-off $99 payment. The DFY offer is a marketing mechanic, not a new product line.

NewWhat Gets Built

One credible DFY ad creative. One landing page or waitlist page. Retarget ad creative with anchor headlines. META pixel on DFY page.

What Not to Do

Do not build a real DFY fulfillment product. Do not promise agency deliverables. Do not let the DFY waitlist grow without a plan to convert those leads to the DIY offer. If someone genuinely wants DFY and has budget, that is a conversation you can have separately. It is not a product line to build in advance.

Strategy Verdict
High upside. Low extra cost. Test the ad sequence first.

The core mechanic is sound. Price anchoring via a competing offer is one of the most reliable conversion levers in direct response. The retarget pool this builds will be warmer and more pre-sold than any cold audience you could buy.

The only real requirement is that the DFY creative looks credible. Everything else runs through the existing funnel with no changes.

Build order: DFY ad creative and landing page first. Let the retarget pool build. Then run the contrast retarget against it. Validate the sequence before touching the landing page.

Build a credible DFY agency ad creative (static or short video)
Build a minimal DFY landing page with waitlist CTA (no fulfillment)
Install META pixel on DFY page and create retarget audience
Build retarget ad creative using exact anchor price in headline
Run sequence and measure CPL and conversion rate vs cold traffic
If sequence converts well, consider adding DFY section to landing page
