The Premise

Cool builds get applause. Painful, specific, useful builds get invoices.

PlainBlack does not ship calculators. We ship tools we would happily use ourselves, aimed at pains people either grind through quietly or pay way too much to solve badly (usually with a fix that comes with new problems). Objects with personality, live meters, shareable artefacts, and the kind of voice that makes a roofer screenshot it to his mate.

The framework is the table. The voice, the specificity, and the speed of execution are the food. AI gets you the framework. Imagination is what makes the framework spread.

GPT thinks PlainBlack voice is a profanity filter. Add some "fucks" and "cunts," recycle a Reddit joke, ship. That is not the voice. The voice is the context, the big picture, and the result behind it. The difference between a selfie reframed as a questionably-copyrighted Instagram post, and a scalable, tangible piece of delivery that actually turns a profit.

Two good cunts with big hearts, big imaginations, and high standards, plus an army of AI death robots we have repurposed to help us help you do better. Every day a build. Once a week a public audit. One capstone that does not try to sell you anything, because the audience already knows where to find us. The whole thing threads back through the first eleven builds, because nothing in the back half exists in a vacuum.

Day 2626
Voice Stack · Part 2

The Voice Fingerprint

And the public roast of three local agencies whose websites sound like printer manuals wearing cologne.

Demo on
Three local marketing agencies' own websites. Logos blurred for the launch post; the URLs are technically retrievable. Bonus round: each agency's own client sites get the same audit, to test whether they practice what they preach.
The toy
Paste a URL or copy. The tool runs four passes side by side:
1 · The Voice Fingerprint. Radial chart: jargon density, hedge-word count, generic-phrase saturation, "could any business say this?" score. Voice Twin from Day 17 is the calibration baseline.
2 · The Slop Detector. Flags keyword-stuffed copy that exists to game SEO but reads as nothing to a human ("end-to-end solutions tailored to your unique business needs across diverse industry verticals"). Counts the words a real customer would skip.
3 · The Technical Audit. Missing alt tags. Missing H1. Missing schema. Lighthouse score (performance, accessibility, SEO). The agency either does not know what they are doing, or does not care.
4 · The Hypocrisy Check. The kicker. Same audit, run on the agency's last five client sites. Did the agency ship for their clients what they could not ship for themselves? Spoiler: not even close.
The artefact
The Voice + Slop + Schema + Hypocrisy report, hosted at plainblackcreative.com/voice-check/[slug] as a permanent interactive page. Demoed on Ian's voice and on three local agencies in the launch post: "We ran this on three local agencies AND their last fifteen client sites. Here is what came back."
Commercial path
The people who fail their own copy, their own tech audit, AND their own client sites come straight to Brand Sprint or Name & Frame. We become the people who measure both voice and execution in the local market. Nobody else is auditing all four passes at once.
Picking a fight with three local agencies in public on a Tuesday is the kind of thing the internet remembers. Picking a fight with them about their own clients' websites is the kind of thing the internet repeats. Done with skill, it positions PB as the only agency willing to be measured. Done with grace, nobody can credibly fight back.
