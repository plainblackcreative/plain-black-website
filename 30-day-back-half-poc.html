The Premise

Cool builds get applause. Painful, specific, useful builds get invoices.

PlainBlack does not ship calculators. We ship tools we would happily use ourselves, aimed at pains people either grind through quietly or pay way too much to solve badly (usually with a fix that comes with new problems). Objects with personality, live meters, shareable artefacts, and the kind of voice that makes a roofer screenshot it to his mate.

The framework is the table. The voice, the specificity, and the speed of execution are the food. AI gets you the framework. Imagination is what makes the framework spread.

GPT thinks PlainBlack voice is a profanity filter. Add some "fucks" and "cunts," recycle a Reddit joke, ship. That is not the voice. The voice is the context, the big picture, and the result behind it. The difference between a selfie reframed as a questionably-copyrighted Instagram post, and a scalable, tangible piece of delivery that actually turns a profit.

Two good cunts with big hearts, big imaginations, and high standards, plus an army of AI death robots we have repurposed to help us help you do better. Every day a build. Once a week a public audit. One capstone that does not try to sell you anything, because the audience already knows where to find us. The whole thing threads back through the first eleven builds, because nothing in the back half exists in a vacuum.

Corrections to the First Draft

Three things the previous version got wrong, owned and fixed.

No pricing reveal as a climax.

The original closing days were "Branded System launch" on Day 13 and "The close" on Day 30. Both were product reveals dressed as content. That breaks the contract the first 29 days quietly built, which is: we give value, no walls, no pitch in the build itself.

The reveal of what's still running, what we shipped, what we learned, and what is now available to buy lives in a Day 37 post, one week later. Different audience permission level. Earned its turn by then.

Meta days, gone.

Five "non-build" days were quietly added by the previous draft (13, 17, 19, 29, 30). Those days have been returned to their original purpose: real builds.

Only three weekly audit days remain: Day 14, Day 21, Day 28. Each one is a numbers report, an honest unwind of the previous week, and a setup for the week ahead. Not a build, not a pitch, just a public audit, including a "Best & Worst AI Chats" segment because we like a running joke.

The Founder Script

Daily and weekly video scripts in your own voice, ready to record. Or ready to hand to your Voice Twin.

Phone
Nobody for the build. Demo it live on Jay's voice, then on a local thought leader's voice, then on a deliberately bland generic voice. Show the difference.
The toy
Three inputs: your business, what you did this week, what you want viewers to do next. The tool generates a 60- to 90-second script in your voice (pulled from your Voice Twin from Day 17), with stage directions, b-roll suggestions, and a recommended hook in the first three seconds. Five script variants per generation. Instant re-roll. Editable inline. Output: a teleprompter-ready script PDF or a JSON payload ready for the next build.
The artefact
The Script Card. Founders post the generated scripts with "this is what AI sounds like when you train it on your own voice." Tomorrow the same founders will be in the comments section of Day 30 going "wait you can do WHAT with these?"
Commercial path
Free at a basic tier (one script per day). Subscription tier for unlimited scripts plus team voices. Bundles with Idea Engine. Plus the obvious Day 30 upsell.
Day 29 is the loaded gun. Day 30 pulls the trigger. The audience does not know yet what the script generator is for. Tomorrow they find out.

The Reveal

Today I Built… Ain.

Jay rips into Ian for not posting a video every day for the entire challenge. Then introduces the AI version of Ian who absolutely should have been doing it all along. We named him Ain. We are five-year-olds and proud of it.

Phone
Ian (already in on the joke). Plus the camera-shy founder, coach, podcaster, agency principal, accountant, lawyer, and consultant who has known for a year that they should be on video and isn't.
The build
A custom HeyGen avatar called Ain, trained on real footage of Ian, calibrated to a slightly exaggerated comic register. The name does the work a disclaimer would have to: nobody mistakes "Ain" for a deepfake when the wordplay is the punchline. Voice cloned from the Day 17 Voice Twin. Script written by the Day 29 Founder Script generator. Ain delivers a 90-second over-the-top tribute to Jay's brilliance, including the line "I should have been doing this since Day 2." Closes by inviting any other camera-shy business owner to get their own.
The callback
Brandon McIndoe shoots the training footage (preferred). Day 6's client returns for the Day 30 build; the 30 days starts referencing itself, like a small cinematic universe. Fallback if Brandon is slammed: Jay shoots on a phone tripod, Brandon gets the credit in the post anyway because the wider play is his too. Nobody plans for this kind of structural recursion in advance. Everybody recognises it when they see it.
The artefact
The video itself. Plus a behind-the-scenes carousel: Jay walks through HeyGen training, the voice tuning, the comic calibration, the script handoff. People who do not buy still learn how the trick is done. PB never gates the workflow.
The productised service
AI Twin by PlainBlack. Setup fee builds the avatar from real footage and a Voice Twin. Recurring fee covers unlimited scripts, monthly renders, and voice updates. Ain is the public proof of concept. The service is the product line that ships from it.
The punchline that lands
The "should have built it Day 2" joke only works because the running tension built up over 29 days. If we had actually built it on Day 2, the joke would not exist. The strategic delay is the value. Land that distinction in the script so neither the audience nor we confuse the bit with actual self-criticism.
Capstone criteria: (1) pays off a running thread, (2) demonstrates the strongest version of the PB thesis, (3) ships something nobody else in the local market has built, (4) creates a productisable next step. Most "biggest build" candidates do one or two. This one does all four. The running joke about Ian not posting videos is the thread that makes the payoff land emotionally instead of just impressively.
