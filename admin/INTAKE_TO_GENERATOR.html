01

Quick start checklist

When a new Web3Forms lead arrives in Gmail:

  1. Open the lead. Read the 6 required fields the customer submitted: business name, owner first name, email, city/suburb, primary service, country.
  2. Spend 5 minutes researching the business. Open their website, their Google Business Profile, their socials. You're looking for: a few brand colours, one direct competitor, the rough scale of the business, anything that helps you write a hero headline that reads personalised rather than generic.
  3. Open admin.plainblackcreative.com. Log in.
  4. Pick the product (top of the form, dropdown). Fill in:
    • The 6 fields from intake (universal section at top)
    • The product-specific fields, with answers from your 5 minutes of research
    • The accent colour pickers, using the client's brand hex from their site or logo
    • System fields auto-fill (slug, playbook ID, month/year, Stripe link). Don't touch.
  5. Click Generate. The output panel shows two random-slug filenames plus drafts of Email 1 (locked link) and Email 2 (unlocked link).
  6. Click Publish. Both files commit and push to the private client repo.
  7. Copy Email 1 into Gmail, send to the lead. Done. Email 2 stays in your drafts until Stripe confirms payment, then you send it.
Total time per lead, once you're warmed up: 10 to 15 minutes.

The 6 intake fields, mapped to the generator

Every Web3Forms lead gives you the same 6 fields. They map identically across all 5 products, with one exception: the customer's "Primary service" answer maps to a different generator field depending on which product you're generating.

For google-reviews specifically, the customer's "Primary service" answer often needs to populate both BUSINESS_TYPE (the short descriptor, e.g. "cafe") and INDUSTRY (the broader category, e.g. "hospitality"). Use judgement.

The generator also asks for OWNER_NAME (full name) on every product. The intake form only collects first name. Either ask the lead in your reply or fill in their first name twice.

03

Research fields per product

Everything below isn't in the intake form. You research it, decide it, or write it. Budget 5 minutes per lead.

90-day-job-pipeline

Just one research field beyond TRADE_TYPE. Easy product to generate.

ai-agents

Six things to figure out:

  • BUSINESS_TYPE, full descriptor of the business
  • TEAM_SIZE, how many people total, including the owner
  • TOP_TIME_SINK, the single biggest manual task eating their week. If you don't know, ask them in your reply email
  • HOURS_SAVED_TARGET, leave at default 10 unless the lead said otherwise
  • CURRENT_TOOLS, what software they already use. Often visible on their site (Stripe footer logo, "Powered by Squarespace", etc.)
  • HERO_OUTCOME_HEADLINE, write a one-sentence personalised headline that ties their top time sink to a weekly hours-saved outcome

google-reviews

The most research-heavy product. Open their Google Business Profile in a tab.

  • BUSINESS_TYPE, INDUSTRY, METRO, descriptors and area
  • CUSTOMERS, the plural noun the owner uses for clients ("guests", "patients", "homeowners"). Often inferable from their site copy
  • GBP_CATEGORY, exact wording from their Google listing, case-sensitive
  • COMPETITOR_NAME, pick one nearby competitor with a similar GBP category
  • CURRENT_RATING, CURRENT_REVIEW_COUNT, read off their Google profile
  • TARGET_RATING, default 4.8, leave alone unless they're already higher
  • TARGET_REVIEW_COUNT, pick a realistic 90-day target, usually current + 50
  • ROUTINE_DAY, ROUTINE_TIME, pick the quietest operational day and a consistent time. For hospitality, often a Monday morning
  • HERO_OUTCOME_HEADLINE, ties their rating gap to a dollar or footfall outcome
  • COMMON_NEGATIVE_1/2/3_LABEL and _RESPONSE, the three most common complaint patterns for their business type, with scripted owner replies. If you can't see negative reviews on their profile, infer from their business type
  • SIDEBAR_STAT_1/2/3_LABEL and _VALUE, three quick-glance metrics for the sidebar, e.g. "Current rating / 4.2 ★", "Venue / Swell & Co.", "Location / Burleigh Heads"

marketing

  • BUSINESS_TYPE
  • PRIMARY_OFFER, the specific thing the ads will promote, not just what the business sells
  • TARGET_CUSTOMER, 1 to 2 sentences covering demographics, location, mindset, pain
  • COMPETITOR_NAME
  • AD_BUDGET_MONTHLY, number only, in their local currency. If the lead didn't mention budget, make a reasonable guess based on business size or ask them
  • AVG_CUSTOMER_VALUE, number only. If unsure, use first-purchase value as a conservative proxy
  • CURRENT_PLATFORMS, where they advertise now. Leave blank if zero
  • HERO_OUTCOME_HEADLINE, ties budget to customer-acquisition outcome

roofing-ai

  • CREW_SIZE, total people on crew including owner
  • PRIMARY_SERVICE, main type of roofing work (already populated from intake's "Primary service" answer)
  • SECONDARY_SERVICES, other services they offer, comma-separated
  • SERVICE_AREA, geographic radius or named region
  • AVG_JOB_VALUE, number only, local currency
  • COMPANY_WEBSITE, full URL
  • GOOGLE_URL, Google Business Profile URL
  • GBP_CATEGORY, exact wording from their listing
  • COMPETITOR_NAME
  • HERO_OUTCOME_HEADLINE, ties their primary service to a pipeline outcome

04

Auto-derived fields, don't touch

The generator fills these in for you. If you see them, leave them alone unless something looks visibly wrong.

FieldHow it's derived
BUSINESS_SLUGFrom BUSINESS_NAME, plus a 6-character random suffix
PLAYBOOK_IDMirrors BUSINESS_SLUG
MONTH_YEARCurrent month and year
STRIPE_PAYMENT_LINKFrom COUNTRY (NZ to NZD link, AU to AUD, others to USD)
Accent colour defaultsEach product has a niche-appropriate default. Override using the picker if the client has a brand colour.
If a customer is from a country other than NZ, AU, or US, the generator defaults to the USD Stripe link. Stripe handles currency conversion at checkout, so this works fine.

05

Common gotchas

Don't paste accent hex with the # symbol The colour picker expects 3ecf8e or #3ecf8e depending on browser. If pasting fails, type the hex directly into the input.
Don't skip the OWNER_NAME field Intake only gives you first name. The field needs a full name or it'll render awkwardly in body copy.
Don't override the Stripe link unless you know why The auto-derived link is correct for 99% of leads. If you do override, double-check you're using the right currency.
Hero headlines that read generic kill the experience The whole product hinges on the customer feeling like the playbook was made for them. If your headline could apply to any other business in the same niche, rewrite it.
Hard refresh the live admin generator with care On Jayden's Mac, Cmd+Shift+R triggers reading mode, not a hard refresh. Use an incognito window, or open DevTools (Cmd+Option+I), right-click the refresh button, pick "Empty Cache and Hard Reload".
The locked URL goes out via Email 1, the unlocked URL via Email 2 Don't mix them up. Email 1 ships at intake, Email 2 only after Stripe confirms.
A customer playbook lives at client.plainblackcreative.com/playbooks/[slug].html Not the public site. If you can't find a customer's file, check the private plainblack-client repo.

07

When to update this doc

Anything that changes the routine. New product launched, new field added to the generator, intake form modified, a recurring gotcha that bit someone twice.

Don't write to it for one-off issues. Don't let it grow past 4 pages.
