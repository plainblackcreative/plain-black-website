We built a blog generator that actually sounds like us

4 min read By PlainBlack 10 May 2026
← Back to Blog

Today I shipped a blog and social media generator we built for ourselves. Internal tool. The 30-day challenge made it obvious I was the bottleneck. Writing daily was killing me. ChatGPT slop wasn't an option because it sounds nothing like us. So we built our own.

What it does

One topic in, four outputs out. Full blog post in our voice. Facebook post. LinkedIn post. Image prompts ready for GPT Image or Midjourney. Alt text for accessibility. Tags auto-generated from the body.

The clever bit: per-field regenerate. Edit the body, click Regenerate on JUST tags. Don't lose the LinkedIn copy you spent ten minutes polishing. Most AI content tools rewrite everything, every time. Ours doesn't.

The difference between "asking ChatGPT to write your posts" and "building a system that knows your voice and ships four platform-specific outputs from one input" is the difference between slop and a tool that actually works.

Real example

I used it this morning to write our response to Namecheap's "5 reasons blogging is still relevant". Whole post, Facebook and LinkedIn copy, tags, image prompts. Read it here.

The bigger question

Do blogs still work in 2026? We don't know yet. The 30-day challenge IS the experiment. 100% organic, SEO-heavy, build-in-public posts. No ad spend. We'll know in 20 days.

If organic flops, the next 30-day challenge is "30 days of ad spend and a holiday". Half joking.

Why this matters

Most small businesses are stuck between two bad options. Either spend hours writing everything yourself, or hand it off to a generic AI tool that produces content that sounds like every other business on the internet.

The third option is better. Build systems that encode your actual voice, your actual principles, your actual edge. Then use those systems to ship faster without sounding like a robot.

We're not saying everyone needs to build their own content generator. But you do need to figure out how to get your voice into whatever system you're using. Whether that's custom instructions in ChatGPT, a carefully tuned Claude prompt, or a purpose-built tool like ours.

The test is simple

If someone who knows your business reads your AI-generated content and can't tell it's AI, you're doing it right. If they can tell, you're making noise.

The 30-day challenge continues. We're testing whether organic content still works in 2026. This tool is how we're going to survive the test without burning out. If you want to follow along or talk about building systems that actually sound like you, get in touch.
