Can you really trust Ian?

3 min read By PlainBlack 30 April 2026
← Back to Blog

Ian published a piece about industries getting hammered by AI in 2026. You might have read it. You might have nodded along. You might have thought, "Wow, Ian really knows his stuff."

Well.

Here's the thing about Ian. He's not wrong. Nearly 30% of structured news stories are now created entirely by AI, and freelance translator earnings have fallen by 35% since 2020. The data checks out. Ian can read a report.

But can you trust him? That's a different question.

What Ian told you

Ian gave you the headline stats. Publishing, journalism, translation. All getting disrupted. Very scary. Much concern. He probably threw in a McKinsey statistic and called it a day.

What Ian didn't tell you is that AI will augment 20% of jobs over the next five years rather than eliminate them, representing a nearly fourfold increase from earlier forecasts. Or that while 85 million jobs will be displaced by 2025, 97 million new roles will emerge, a net positive of 12 million positions globally.

Funny how Ian skipped that part.

The bit about trust

Look, Ian's heart is probably in the right place. He wants you to know AI is coming. He wants you to feel prepared. Noble.

But here's what actually matters for your small business: technology delivers only about 20% of an initiative's value, while the other 80% comes from redesigning work so you can focus on what drives impact.

Ian didn't mention that either.

The real question

It's not whether AI will disrupt your industry. It will. The question is whether you're building the muscle to adapt faster than your competitors.

What you actually need to know

First, 40% of enterprise applications will leverage task-specific AI agents by 2026, compared to less than 5% in 2025. That's not a trend. That's a land grab.

Second, the businesses winning right now aren't the ones reading Ian's doom-and-gloom industry reports. They're the ones running scam defence playbooks, building AI literacy into their teams, and treating this like the operational shift it is.

Third, if you're still waiting for someone to tell you it's safe to start, you've already lost six months.

Ian gave you fear. We're giving you the playbook. Check out our Scam Defence resource in the sidebar if you want to stop reading and start building.

Can you trust Ian? Sure. Just don't mistake his article for a strategy.
