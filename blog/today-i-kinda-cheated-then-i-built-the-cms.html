Today I Kinda Cheated. Then I Built the CMS.

4 min read By PlainBlack 6 May 2026
← Back to Blog

I'm going to be honest. Today I almost cheated.

Day 6 of the 30-day build challenge was meant to be the McIndoe Media Works reveal.

The site was already live. The brand had two modes. The hero was working. The BEEF.PROD alter ego had its own energy. I had enough to write a tidy little recap, publish it, tick the box, and walk away.

Then Brandon came over.

We sat down to look at the site and, within about ten minutes, the "quick review" became a full creative spiral. Tweaks, new ideas, better section logic, content plans, future trips, CrossFit events, Europe, fishing, brand work, thumbnails, reels, the lot.

Good work. Useful work.

But not the kind of work that gives you a clean blog headline at 4pm.

So I had three options.

  • Use the site I already built and pretend that was today's build.
  • Delete the blog idea and hope no one noticed.
  • Solve the actual problem sitting underneath the website.

The problem was not "does Brandon have a cool site?"

He does.

The problem was, who keeps it alive when he is busy?

Brandon is a videographer, photographer and designer. If you have ever worked around video editing, you know how intense it is. The last thing he needs after filming an event is another complicated admin job.

And selfishly, I do not want to become the guy who has to manually swap every image, thumbnail, link and caption every time he finishes a shoot.

He is heading to Australia to film CrossFit events. After that, Europe. More footage, more stories, more work worth showing.

I do not want to wait two months to promote the finished product because the website needs me to update it.

So today's actual build became the thing that lets the site keep growing after launch.

The first version of a CMS.

Not a giant enterprise dashboard. Not another platform for him to learn.

A simple web-based content admin that opens straight onto the job he actually needs to do.

Pick the empty Work card. Paste or upload an image. Add a title, subtitle, alt text and click URL. Preview it inside the site layout. Save a draft or queue the update.

That is the whole idea.

The important part was the preview

At first, the CMS had a "Site" preview that loaded the actual live site in an iframe. Sounds clever, until you realise it was showing the published site, not the change Brandon was trying to make.

So I replaced it with a local mock of the Work section.

Now when Brandon pastes a direct image URL, the selected empty card changes inside the section preview, surrounded by the other cards. He is not guessing what the update will look like. He can see it.

That is the difference between a CMS that technically works and a CMS someone might actually use.

I also fixed the paste behaviour properly.

Direct image URLs now render instantly in both the Card preview and the Site preview. Instagram links get saved as click URLs instead of pretending to be thumbnails. Non-image URLs explain what went wrong instead of failing silently.

And because this is still the first version, the buttons tell the truth.

Save Draft says it saved locally. Update Website says the demo update is queued, preview only, not published live.

No fake magic. No pretend backend.

That can come next.

Today I nearly used the work I had already done and called it a day.

Instead, I shipped the first version of the thing that keeps Brandon's site alive while he is out making the work.

Because a website launch is a moment.

A website that stays useful needs a workflow.

So no, I did not cheat.

I just nearly did.
