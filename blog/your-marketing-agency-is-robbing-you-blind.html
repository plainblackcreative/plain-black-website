Your Marketing Agency Is Robbing You Blind

4 min read By PlainBlack 1 April 2026
← Back to Blog

Let's talk about the marketing agency racket.

You're paying $2,000 to $5,000 a month. Maybe more. For what? Monthly reporting decks with vanity metrics. Vague promises about "brand awareness". A few social posts that took someone 20 minutes to write. Maybe some Google Ads they set and forgot three months ago.

Do the math. That's $24,000 to $60,000 a year. For work that, with the right playbook, you could execute yourself in a weekend and maintain in a few hours a month.

The Agency Model Is Broken for Small Business

Here's what most agencies won't tell you: the entire retention model is built on information asymmetry. They keep you dependent. They use jargon. They make simple things sound complex so you keep paying.

META ads? Google Ads? Budget allocation? These aren't rocket science. They're learnable systems. But agencies profit when you believe you can't do it yourself.

The truth is brutal: most agencies are charging you thousands for work that an in-house person with a decent playbook could handle for a fraction of the cost.

What You're Actually Paying For

Break down that $3,500 monthly retainer:

  • Strategy session: One hour. Maybe.
  • Ad setup and monitoring: Two hours if they're being thorough.
  • Content creation: Three to five social posts, written by a junior who Googled your industry yesterday.
  • Reporting: Automated dashboards dressed up in branded PDFs.
  • Account management: Email responses and the occasional Zoom call.

That's maybe 10 to 15 hours of actual work. You're paying $200 to $350 per hour for tasks that don't require that rate.

The Real Cost

What Actually Moves the Needle

You don't need an agency. You need a system. A repeatable framework that covers:

  • META ads that actually convert
  • Google Ads setup without the waste
  • Budget allocation that makes sense for your revenue
  • Content strategy that isn't just posting for the sake of posting
  • AI tools that do the heavy lifting
  • A 90-day launch plan you can execute immediately
  • KPIs that matter, not vanity metrics
  • Automations that save you hours every week

You Don't Need Hand-Holding

The agency model assumes you're incapable. That you need constant check-ins, strategy sessions, and someone to hold your hand through basic campaign setup.

You don't.

What you need is clarity. A proven system. The confidence to execute it yourself. And maybe a few hours on a weekend to get it done.

Most small business marketing isn't complex. It's been deliberately overcomplicated by people who profit from your confusion.
