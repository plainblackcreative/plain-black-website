We could give you the polished version. Agency-level results. Transparent pricing. Creative solutions for ambitious businesses. All true. But if you're actually trying to decide whether to work with us, you deserve the full story, not the brochure.

So here it is.

We Taught Ourselves Everything from the Internet

Ian and Jayden didn't come up through traditional agency paths. No degrees in marketing or design. No internships at large studios. We learned by doing: reading, watching, building things, breaking them, working out what went wrong, and trying again. We built skills because we needed them, not because we were studying toward a credential.

That matters because it means we learned what actually works. Not what works in theory, not what professors teach, but what moves the needle for real businesses trying to grow with limited budgets and limited time. We also made every expensive mistake along the way, which means we know exactly which ones to help you avoid.

We kept learning because the industry never stops changing. What worked for websites and social media five years ago is largely irrelevant today. We stayed current because we had to, and we're genuinely interested in this stuff, which helps.

We Think Like Business Owners, Not Agency Operators

This is the real difference. A lot of agencies approach client work as a product delivery exercise. You want a website, they build a website. You want social media content, they produce social media content. The brief gets executed, the invoice gets sent, the relationship ends.

We come at it differently because we're business owners ourselves. When we look at your situation, we're thinking about ROI, not just deliverables. We're thinking about what you need to grow, not just what you asked for. We'll tell you when we think you're spending money in the wrong place, even if the right place means less work for us.

We also pick our clients carefully. Not because we're precious about it, but because we want to be genuinely enthusiastic about every project we take on. When we care about a business, the work is better. That's not a line; it's just how it works. We'd rather work with ten businesses we're excited about than thirty we're indifferent to.

The Pricing Philosophy

The internet changed the cost of knowledge. Everything we know, we learned from freely available resources. The skills took time and effort to develop, but the underlying information was never behind a paywall. We think that changes what's reasonable to charge.

Agencies that quote $5,000 for a website that could genuinely be built well for $1,500 are charging for mystery, not skill. We charge for skill, experience, and outcomes. Our prices reflect what things actually cost to do well, not what the market will bear from someone who doesn't know any better.

One thing we learned the hard way: trying to do everything yourself to save money usually costs more in the long run, in time, in mistakes, and in the opportunity cost of not focusing on what actually grows your business. We're not saying hire us for everything. We're saying know which things to delegate and which to own yourself.

The Catch

There is one. We choose our clients. Not based on budget or industry, but based on fit. We want to work with good people who have businesses that provide genuine value. We want to be excited about your project, because when we're not, the work suffers and so does the relationship.

If you're a good operator who takes what you do seriously and wants a creative partner who will do the same, we're probably a good fit. If you want the cheapest possible option and don't care much about quality, we're not the right choice and we'd rather say so upfront.

How to find out if we're a fit

Get in touch. Tell us what you're working on. We'll have a straightforward conversation about whether we can help and what that looks like. No hard sell, no proposal you didn't ask for, no six-week discovery process before we tell you anything useful.

If it's a good fit, we'll know quickly. If it's not, we'll tell you that too, and probably point you toward someone who's a better match.
