Eight decades on the same patch

The Eagles have been playing cricket out of 31–49 Mersey Main Rd since 1945 — first as a country side, now as a full senior and junior club with a proper community hall attached.

Family-friendly is the through-line. Members keep playing as they get older, juniors come up through the grades, and the clubrooms double as a hireable venue that funds the parts of the club a sub fee can’t.

  • Senior & junior teams playing out of the Spreyton ground
  • Community hall available for hire (birthdays, functions, fundraisers)
  • Long-running local sponsor list — Marion, Speedy, and other Devonport-area businesses

How GivesBack would run for the Eagles

The club drops their custom GivesBack link into the FB page (1K followers), the season’s newsletter, and the back of the clubrooms hire-pack. No new platform to learn, no admin overhead.

Across a season, a handful of supporters click through and enquire about PlainBlack work for their own businesses — the cafe owner whose kid plays U14s, the tradie who hires the hall for his daughter’s 21st, the parent who runs a hardware shop in Devonport.

Three of those enquiries become paid projects. A Brand Sprint for a new business in town, a Google Reviews Playbook for the local sparkie, and a website + logo package for a tradie expanding into Launceston.

The club gets 10% of every project value paid back into the Eagles’ account. No invoicing, no chasing, no paperwork. The funds line up nicely with junior gear orders and the next round of nets maintenance.
