Why this exists

The most profitable email you send this month might be the one ending something.

Owners stay in bad-fit clients, drift through dead retainers, and forget to cancel subscriptions because the email is uncomfortable to write. Not because the decision is hard. Because the wording is.

The exit email is the smallest unit of business hygiene. Get it written, send it once, leave the follow-up loaded for the inevitable "wait, can we discuss?" reply. Then put the calendar reminder somewhere you’ll see it before the renewal hits.

Three artefacts because that’s the full shape of the job. Most "email generator" tools give you one. The push-back is where these conversations actually fall apart.

Want a hand setting things up so you don’t need to write these as often?

The tool writes the exit. The system stops the bleed at source: contracts with sensible exit clauses, subscriptions you actually use, clients who weren’t bad-fit to begin with.
