Brand Audit & Identity System
Linktree — Free Canva — Free ChatGPT — Free
Goal: Before posting a single thing, your band needs a consistent identity across every platform. Same photo, same bio, same link. Consistency is what makes people remember you. Inconsistency is why they don't.

📌 What to Sort First

  • Write the one-line pitch: "[Band name] — [genre] cover band. The best songs. Available for gigs and private events."
  • Choose one band photo and use it everywhere — no variation
  • Write a standard 3-sentence bio and copy-paste it identically to every platform
  • Pick one booking contact email and use it consistently

🔗 Bio Link Setup

  • Set up a free Linktree at linktr.ee/yourbandname
  • Add three links: booking email, Facebook page, best video clip
  • Use this single URL in the bio field of every platform you're on
  • Update it when gig announcements go live — it's your one source of truth
Sanity check: Google your band name right now. Does every result show the same photo, the same description, and the same contact? If not, fix the inconsistencies before moving to Section 2.

Instagram — From Zero to Gig Machine
Instagram — Free CapCut — Free Meta Business Suite — Free
Why Instagram: Reels are where new fans discover live music now. A 30-second clip from soundcheck can reach 10,000+ people in your city who have never heard of your band. That is simply not possible on Facebook in 2025.

⚙️ Account Setup

  • Create as a Creator profile — not personal, not business
  • Username matches your band name on every other platform
  • Band photo, one-line bio, Linktree URL in bio link field
  • Category: Musician/Band
  • Tag venues in your first gig posts to get on their radar

🎬 Reel Strategy

  • Any live clip over 15 seconds goes up as a Reel — not a regular post
  • 30-45 seconds is the sweet spot, start at the most energetic moment
  • Film vertical (portrait) when possible — gets more reach
  • Caption: venue, city, vibe. Short and direct.
  • 5-8 specific hashtags — not 30 generic ones
Don't boost posts until you have at least 10 posts up and some organic traction. Boosting a new account wastes money. Build the account first, spend later.
🛠️ Recording and posting your first Reel at a gig
▼ Show steps
1
Film at soundcheck or during the set
Prop your phone against a monitor or ask someone to hold it. Film 45-60 seconds of one song. Vertical is preferred but horizontal works too.
2
No editing app needed for the first one
Open Instagram, tap +, select Reel, choose the clip. Trim to 30-45 seconds starting at the most energetic moment in the clip.
3
Add cover image, caption and hashtags
Choose the best frame as the cover. Caption: "[Band name] live at [venue], [city]. Classic rock done properly." Then add 6-8 targeted hashtags.
4
Post within 2 hours of the gig ending
Recency matters. Don't sit on it. The algorithm rewards posts published close to when the content happened.
5
Tag and engage
Tag the venue. Tag band members. Reply to every comment in the first hour — engagement in the first hour is a direct signal to the algorithm to show the post to more people.
Note: Phone-quality video is fine. A slightly shaky clip posted the night of the gig beats a polished video posted two weeks later. Recency beats production quality every time.
Common Instagram Mistakes
  • Only posting gig announcements — mix in clips, crowd shots, throwbacks. Announcements alone get almost no reach.
  • Using 30 hashtags — 6-8 specific ones beats a wall of generic tags.
  • Not replying to comments — engagement in the first hour is what tells the algorithm to show your post to more people.
  • Going quiet between gigs — post at least twice a week even if it's just a throwback clip.

TikTok — The Unexpected Goldmine
TikTok — Free CapCut — Free
Why TikTok works for cover bands: Gen Z is obsessed with classic rock. The "older band plays legendary song" format is one of the most reliably viral things on the platform. You don't need to dance. You just need to play.

⚙️ Account Setup

  • Download TikTok, sign up with the band email
  • Same username, same photo, same bio as Instagram
  • Post at least 3 times in week one so the algorithm learns
  • Cross-post every Instagram Reel — no separate content needed

🎬 What Gets Views

  • Crowd reaction format: film the audience singing along. 15-30 seconds. Gets 3-8x more reach than stage-only footage.
  • Caption style is more casual than Instagram
  • 5 targeted hashtags is enough
  • Post within 2 hours of the gig — recency matters on TikTok too
