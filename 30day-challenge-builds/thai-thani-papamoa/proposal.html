The problem with the menu on the table.

Most customers walk in and order what they always order. Pad Thai. Chicken stir-fry. The safe one. The one they can picture. With a printed menu, they're choosing from words, and most people can't picture the difference between Phad See Eew and Phad Kra Prow and Phad Nam Mum Hoy after the third one.

"If I can't picture it, I won't order it. So I order what I always order. The kitchen never gets to show me what they actually do best."

Average ticket size goes down because the customer plays it safe. The kitchen gets less interesting orders. Repeat customers eat the same thing every time. The dishes you're proudest of stay on the menu, occasionally ordered.

Switching to a QR-code menu doesn't fix this. Most QR menus are just a PDF on the phone. Same wall of words, same default order. We built a different one.

What's actually in the tool.

Eight built-in features that work together. Each one is small. Together they shift how customers order.

📸

Real photos on every dish

The biggest reason customers default to safe orders is that they can't picture the food. We fix that with proper food photography on every card. Production deployment includes a half-day shoot at the restaurant.

✨

"Help me decide" recommender

Three short questions: how hungry, how spicy, what mood. The chef picks a dish for them, with a real photo and a one-line recommendation. Bakes confidence into the unfamiliar dishes.

Try the recommender
🥜

Allergen-aware menu

Customer ticks peanut, shellfish, gluten, dairy etc once at the start. Dishes that contain those ingredients are visibly flagged or dimmed throughout the menu. The kitchen sees the allergen list with the order.

🍹

Smart upsell prompts

After a main is added to the order, a small carousel suggests sides and drinks that pair well. Hunger-aware so a "starving" customer sees a starter prompt; a "snack" customer just gets offered a drink. Won't re-suggest things already in the cart.

🤙

Send to kitchen, call your server

Order goes through. Customer sees a confirmation code in your brand. Server is pinged automatically. Counter mode shows a code instead, for takeaway orders.

🔄

Second-scan, "what next?" loop

Halfway through dinner they scan again. The tool recognises this, skips the welcome, shows: order another drink, call the waiter, order more food, leave a Google review, or share to socials. Repeat-order surface area.

⭐

Built-in Google review prompt

The "leave a review" button on the post-meal screen links straight to your Google Business listing. Reviews from happy guests at the table beat reviews chased over email a week later.

🎉

#ForTheGram share flow

One tap takes a meal photo, applies your tags (@thaithanipapamoa plus a curated hashtag list), and shares to Instagram or Facebook. Social signal piggybacking on a moment customers already want to share.

🛠️

Self-serve admin panel

You control your own menu. Add a dish, change a price, swap a photo, mark today's special, hide an item that's run out. All from your phone or laptop, no coding, no PlainBlack ticket. Changes are live in seconds.

You stay in control of the menu.

The admin panel is part of the deal. You shouldn't have to email anyone to fix a typo or change a price. Everything that customers see, you can edit yourself.

🍜

Add or remove dishes

New dish on the menu? Add it: name, description, price, photo, category, allergen tags, spice level. Saved and live straight away. Pulled an item? One click hides it from the menu without deleting the data.

💲

Prices, sizes, specials

Change a price in two taps. Add a small / large variant. Mark a dish as today's special with a closing time. Flag chef's picks. Bulk-adjust prices across a category if you need to nudge everything up by 5%.

🖼️

Photos, on the fly

Snap a new photo on your phone, upload it, the dish card updates. Take a better one next week, swap it in. The menu stays current without another shoot.

🏷️

Allergens & dietary tags

Mark dishes vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free. Flag what contains peanut, shellfish, fish, soy, egg. The customer-facing allergen filter respects whatever you set, immediately.

⏸️

"86 the duck"

Run out of an ingredient mid-service? Disable the affected dishes from the kitchen with one tap. They go grey on the menu with a "back tomorrow" note. No customers ordering things you can't deliver.

Why it lifts the average ticket.

Three things change when the customer can picture the food and feels guided rather than overwhelmed.

01

Confidence drives spend

Restaurants with photo menus consistently earn higher average ticket sizes than text-only ones. The customer says yes to a dish they otherwise wouldn't have ordered, because they can see what it is.

02

Upsell happens automatically

The "want a side with that?" prompt fires at the right moment, on every order. No staff has to remember to ask. Even a 30% accept rate on sides and drinks moves the average meaningfully.

03

Repeat customers try new dishes

The recommender's job is to nudge customers into the part of the menu they wouldn't otherwise visit. Same regular customer, slightly more interesting order, slightly bigger ticket.

Coexisting with OrderMeal, not replacing it.

You're already on OrderMeal for delivery and online orders. We don't want to touch that. The tool we built lives in a different lane.

Two systems, two lanes.

OrderMeal stays as is

Online ordering. Delivery. Customer at home browsing your menu, wanting it dropped off. Payments, dispatch, delivery integrations. None of that changes. Your existing OrderMeal listing keeps doing what it's already doing.

The new tool covers in-store

Customer sitting at one of your tables. Or standing at the counter waiting to order takeaway. Different context, different experience. The QR code on the table opens this tool, not OrderMeal. The QR at the counter does the same.

Future state (Phase 2): if a customer scans this QR from outside the restaurant, the tool detects geolocation and asks: "Order online for delivery instead?" with a deep link straight to your OrderMeal page. The two systems become one front door.

What production deployment includes.

The version on your phone is the working tool. To go live properly, here's what we'd do.

  • Half-day food photo shoot. Every dish on the menu, shot in your space, in your light. The single biggest visual upgrade and the line item we don't compromise on.
  • Kitchen-send wiring. Right now the tool generates an order code and shows it on screen. We wire the actual delivery: SMS to a kitchen phone, email to a printer queue, or a small staff-side dashboard that pings on every new order. Your call which fits your kitchen.
  • QR code generation, printing, and laminating. One QR per table, one for the counter. Branded card stock with your logo. We deliver them ready to place.
  • Brand polish round. Final pass on colours, fonts, micro-copy, the chef-says quote pool, the recommender's personality. Tuned to how you actually talk about your food.
  • Allergen audit. Walk through your menu with you (or with the chef) and confirm every flag. Shellfish, peanut, gluten, dairy, soy, egg, fish, vegetarian, vegan. Production tool ships with no mistakes.
  • Go-live + first 30 days of tuning. Watch how customers actually use it. Tweak the recommender weights, swap any dishes that aren't landing, adjust upsell rules based on accept rates. The tool gets better as we see real orders flow through it.
  • Hosting on PlainBlack infrastructure. Your URL stays the same forever. Custom domain (e.g. order.thaithanipapamoa.co.nz) if you want one. Updates ship instantly when we change anything.
  • Self-serve admin panel. Add or remove dishes, change prices, swap photos, mark today's special, "86" an item that's run out, manage allergen tags, see which dishes are selling. All from your phone or laptop. No coding, no PlainBlack ticket. Detailed in the admin section above.

What's coming next.

Phase 1 is what's on your phone right now. Here's the runway, in order of impact.

2
Phase 2 · ~ 4 weeks after go-live

Geo-aware "order online" mode

If someone scans the QR but isn't physically at Thai Thani (sharing it with a friend, finding it on socials), the tool offers to take them to OrderMeal for delivery instead. Three buttons: Order now, I'm on the way, Order online for delivery. Bridges in-store and online without confusing the customer.

3
Phase 3 · second month

"Popular with recent guests" goes real

Right now the tool shows a "popular" counter on certain dishes that updates while customers browse. We hook the counter to actual placed orders, so the most-ordered dishes in the last 7 days bubble up automatically. The badge becomes real data, not editorial.

4
Phase 4 · third month

Repeat-customer recognition

If a phone has scanned before, the tool greets them by their last order ("Welcome back. Want the Phad Thai again?") and skips the recommender on subsequent visits. Loyal customers feel known. New ones still get the full treatment.

5
Phase 5 · later

Optional in-tool payment

If you want to skip the "settle at the counter" step entirely, we can add Stripe payment in the tool. Customer pays before they leave the table. You keep your existing eftpos for the people who prefer it. Optional, not a precondition.
