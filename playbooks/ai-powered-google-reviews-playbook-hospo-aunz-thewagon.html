Managing Two Google Business Profiles Without Losing Your Mind
One system for Papamoa and Whakatane. Who manages what, and how.
Google Business Profile · Free
Pending
Goal: Set up both Wagon profiles so Catherine, Sammy, and Danielle each know exactly which profile they're responsible for, with notifications on so nothing gets missed at either location.

Suggested ownership split for The Wagon team

  • Papamoa profile: assign primary ownership to whoever is most present at that location day-to-day
  • Whakatane profile: assign to the other location lead
  • Danielle as backup manager on both: sees all reviews, steps in when needed
  • Each person gets email notifications to their own inbox for their location
  • Weekly Sunday check-in between the three of you: "anything tricky come in this week?"

Profile basics to check on both listings

  • Business name consistent: "The Wagon" (not "The Wagon Burgers" or variations)
  • Primary category: "Hamburger Restaurant" for both
  • Hours accurate for each location including NZ public holidays
  • Menu link active: thewagon.co.nz linked on both profiles
  • At least 10 photos per profile: burgers, sides, interior, team, atmosphere
Common mistake for multi-location businesses: Both profiles get linked to one person's email, that person gets overwhelmed, nothing gets responded to. Split the responsibility and both profiles stay healthy.
Step-by-step: Set up notifications on both Wagon profiles ▼
1
Go to business.google.com. If both locations are under one account, you'll see them both in the dashboard. If they're separate, log into each.
2
For each profile: click Settings, then Notifications. Turn on email alerts for all new reviews. Assign to the correct team member's inbox for that location.
3
Download the Google Business Profile app on each team member's phone. Sign in and enable push notifications.
4
Under Users on each profile, add the other two family members as managers. Everyone can see everything. Primary responder is whoever owns that location.
5
Set a shared Sunday evening calendar reminder: "Wagon reviews check." Five minutes. Both profiles. Anything that needs a response gets flagged for Monday.

The Wagon's Response Framework
A decision tree for every review type across both locations. Consistent voice, clear rules.
ChatGPT · FreeNotion · Free
Pending
Goal: Build a shared response framework for The Wagon so Catherine, Sammy, and Danielle all respond in the same voice, with the same standards, regardless of who's handling a particular review that week.

The three review types for The Wagon

  • Positive (4-5 stars): warm, personal, reference something specific from the review
  • Neutral (3 stars): acknowledge the gap, invite them back, no defensiveness
  • Negative (1-2 stars): own it, short apology without excuses, take it offline
  • Stars only, no text: still respond. A short thank you shows you noticed.

Response time targets

  • Negative: within 24 hours. Every time. This is the one that matters most.
  • Neutral: within 48 hours
  • Positive: within 72 hours. Sooner builds more goodwill.
  • Family rule: if one person can't respond this week, another steps in. Don't leave it.
Voice consistency matters: If Catherine responds one week and Sammy responds the next, the profile shouldn't feel like two different businesses. Section 06 gives you the ChatGPT prompt that keeps the voice consistent across all three of you.
Step-by-step: Build The Wagon shared response policy ▼
1
Open a shared Google Doc or Notion page titled "The Wagon Review Policy." All three team members should have access.
2
Create three sections: Positive, Neutral, Negative. Write one opening line per type that sounds like The Wagon. Warm, direct, family business energy.
3
Add response time targets as rules, not guidelines: "Negative reviews: 24 hours. No exceptions. If you're swamped, tag the group chat."
4
Add a backup rule: "If the primary person for that location can't respond this week, Danielle steps in."
5
Save the doc link in the Sunday calendar reminder so it's always two taps away during the weekly check-in.

04
Handling The Wagon's Negative Reviews the Right Way
Consistency complaints, dry patties, voucher disputes. All handled.
ChatGPT · Free
Pending
Goal: Give the team a proven structure for every negative review at The Wagon so the response is calm, human, and turns every future customer who reads it into a supporter rather than a sceptic.

The four-part structure for negative reviews

  • 1. Name the specific complaint. Don't be generic.
  • 2. Apologise without excuses or justifications.
  • 3. Offer a direct contact to follow up.
  • 4. Keep it under 100 words. Short signals confidence.

The Wagon's common negatives, handled

  • Dry or inconsistent patties: "That's not the burger we want to serve. Consistency is something we're actively working on and this tells us we're not there yet."
  • Voucher not honoured: "We understand that was frustrating. Please reach out to us directly and we'll make it right."
  • Wait time or service issue: "We didn't deliver the experience we aim for. Please come back and give us the chance to show you what The Wagon is really like."
The voucher situation: If a negative review about an unaccepted voucher is still live on either profile, address it publicly now. A calm, accountable response tells future customers you're fair to deal with. Ignoring it tells them you're not.
Step-by-step: Clear The Wagon's unanswered negative reviews ▼
1
Filter both profiles by lowest rating first. Find every 1 and 2-star review without a response on both Papamoa and Whakatane.
2
For each review: write one sentence summarising the specific complaint. Don't generalise.
3
Open ChatGPT and use The Wagon prompt from Section 06. Paste in the review. Get a draft.
4
Edit the draft so it sounds like the family. If it sounds like a PR statement, rewrite it until it sounds like Catherine, Sammy, or Danielle would actually say it.
5
Post it. Move to the next. Clear both profiles in one session today.

Getting More 5-Star Reviews at Both Wagon Locations
The QR system and the one-liner that turns happy customers into advocates.
Google Business Profile · FreeQR Code Generator · Free
Pending
Goal: Build a consistent, low-pressure system at both Papamoa and Whakatane that generates more genuine 5-star reviews every week, without the team feeling like they're begging for them.

When to ask at The Wagon

  • When a table tells you the Fully Loaded or Flying Beast was amazing
  • When a regular comes in for their Wednesday wings and mentions how good they are
  • Via QR code on every table, the counter, and takeaway bags at both locations
  • Not during a peak service rush when the team is flat out
  • Not immediately after a complaint or a wait

What to say at The Wagon (word for word)

  • "Glad you loved it. A Google review genuinely helps a family business like ours. QR code's on the table if you get a sec."
  • One line. Zero pressure. The "family business" framing works especially well in regional NZ.
  • Train all three team members to use the same line naturally.
  • Two separate QR codes: one for Papamoa profile, one for Whakatane profile.
Two QR codes, not one: Each location needs its own QR code linking to its own Google profile. A Papamoa customer leaving a review on the Whakatane profile confuses both profiles. Generate and deploy them separately.
Step-by-step: Create and deploy QR codes at both Wagon locations ▼
1
Log into business.google.com. Go to The Wagon Papamoa profile. Find "Get more reviews" and copy the short review link.
2
Go to qr-code-generator.com. Paste the Papamoa link. Generate and download. Label this file "QR-Papamoa."
3
Repeat for The Wagon Whakatane profile. Download and label "QR-Whakatane."
4
Print and deploy each code only at its own location. Tables, counter, and takeaway bags. Label: "Loved your burger? Leave us a Google review."
5
Test both QR codes yourself. Confirm each goes to the correct location's review screen.

08
Handling Fake and Malicious Reviews Across Both Locations
How to spot them, report them, and respond to them without making things worse.
Google Business Profile · Free
Pending
Goal: Give the team a clear, calm process for identifying and dealing with reviews that look suspicious, at either Papamoa or Whakatane, without escalating publicly or letting them drag down either rating.

Signs a review may not be genuine

  • Reviewer has no profile photo and zero other reviews
  • Review describes something that never happened or doesn't match The Wagon's setup
  • Posted at a time that doesn't match service hours at either location
  • Multiple 1-star reviews arrive within a short window with vague complaints
  • Language reads nothing like how actual Bay of Plenty customers write

What Google will and won't remove

  • Google will remove: spam, off-topic, fake profiles, conflict of interest, policy violations
  • Google will remove: reviews that include false factual claims with evidence
  • Google will NOT remove: reviews just because they are negative, unfair, or hurtful
  • Both profiles can be monitored under the same Business Manager account
How to respond publicly to a suspicious review: "We don't have any record of a visit matching this description at either of our locations. If this was a genuine experience at The Wagon, please reach out to us directly so we can look into it and make it right." Calm. Professional. Let future customers read the context.
Step-by-step: Flag and report a suspicious review at either location ▼
1
Go to the relevant location's Google Maps listing and find the review.
2
Click the three dots (...) next to the review. Select "Report review." Choose the most accurate reason.
3
Post the calm public response immediately. Don't wait for Google to decide. Future customers need to see you handling it professionally.
4
If not removed within a week, go to business.google.com and use the "Request review removal" tool under Reviews for that location's profile.
5
Screenshot everything: the review, the reviewer's profile, your report, and timestamps. Save to a shared folder. If escalation is needed, you'll have the evidence.

Google Sheets · FreeGoogle Business Profile · Free
Pending
Goal: Build one shared Google Sheet that tracks both Papamoa and Whakatane side by side, so the whole team can see progress, catch problems early, and celebrate when both locations hit their targets.

What to track weekly for both locations

  • Date of weekly check
  • Papamoa: rating, total reviews, new this week, responded, response rate %
  • Whakatane: rating, total reviews, new this week, responded, response rate %
  • Notes: anything notable at either location this week
  • Takes 2 minutes per location once the habit is established

What success looks like for The Wagon

  • Response rate above 90% at both locations every week
  • Both ratings stable or improving over 90 days
  • No negative review unanswered for more than 24 hours at either location
  • 3 to 5 new reviews per month per location from the QR system
  • Both locations trending toward 4.5 stars within 6 months
When to investigate: If one location's rating dips while the other holds steady, the issue is location-specific. That's a service or consistency problem at that site, not a review management problem. Address the root cause, not just the responses.
Step-by-step: Set up The Wagon's shared tracking sheet ▼
1
Go to sheets.google.com. Create a new spreadsheet. Title it "The Wagon Review Tracker." Share it with all three team members.
2
Create two tabs: "Papamoa" and "Whakatane." Each tab has the same columns: Date, Rating, Total Reviews, New This Week, Responded, Response Rate %, Notes.
3
Fill in today's baseline row on both tabs. Check current stats at business.google.com for each location.
4
Update both tabs every Sunday as part of the routine from Section 09.
5
After 8 weeks, add a line chart on each tab showing the rating over time. Two lines going up is the proof the system is working across both locations.
