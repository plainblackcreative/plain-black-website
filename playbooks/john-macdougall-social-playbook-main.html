Make MLM Modern Again
Reframe the business so it doesn't make people cringe
Pending
MLM has an image problem. Influencers mock it. Reddit tears it apart. Documentaries frame it as a scam. The people who dismiss it loudest have never looked at a product like Zinzino's. Your job is not to defend MLM. Your job is to make the business model invisible and the product and results undeniable. When someone buys BalanceOil+ and retests their Omega ratio, they don't care about the business model. They care that it worked.
Stop using these words in your content immediately: "MLM," "network marketing," "join my team," "business opportunity," "passive income," "financial freedom," "be your own boss." These phrases trigger the same part of the brain as spam. Replace them entirely.
R The reframe that actually works in 2025

You are not a network marketer. You are a health advocate who recommends products that have measurably improved your own results. You happen to earn a commission when others do the same. That is the same model as every influencer, affiliate marketer, and financial advisor on the planet. Own it.

  • ✓Never lead with the opportunity. Lead with the product and results.
  • ✓Never pitch in public posts. Public posts build audience. DMs close.
  • ✓Position yourself as curious, not salesy: "I'm sharing what worked for me."
  • ✓Talk about the science of omega ratios, not the product name, first.
  • ✓Use "I partner with Zinzino" not "I sell for Zinzino"
DIY Steps How to write your personal positioning statement ▼
  1. Open a notes app or Claude.ai. Type: "Help me write a one-sentence positioning statement for someone who advocates for science-backed omega supplements, has personally tested and improved their Omega-6:3 ratio, and earns income by referring others. No MLM language. No hype."
  2. Take the output and rewrite it in your own voice. It should sound like you in conversation, not a LinkedIn bio.
  3. Example: "I spent years in health and wellness before I found something with a blood test to back it up. Now I help people find out what their body is actually missing, starting with a number most doctors don't check."
  4. Put this statement in your LinkedIn About section, your Facebook bio, and the first 30 seconds of any intro video you make.
  5. Sanity check: read it aloud. If it sounds like a pitch, rewrite it. If it sounds like a conversation, it's ready.
A AI vs. MLM: why you actually win

AI is replacing generic content. AI cannot replace a trusted human recommendation from someone whose results are documented. That is your entire competitive advantage. Lean into it hard.

  • ✓Post your own BalanceTest result (before and after) as social proof content
  • ✓Use AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT) to help you write content faster, not to sound like AI
  • ✓Influencers promote products they've never used. You have test results. Use that contrast.

Your Personal Brand on LinkedIn
Build credibility with professionals who actually have disposable income
Pending
LinkedIn is the best organic platform for selling health products to professionals aged 35 to 60. These are people with money, health concerns, and a respect for science-backed claims. Zinzino's test-based model is perfectly positioned for this audience. Your current LinkedIn profile (linkedin.com/in/john-n-macdougall) is a starting point. This section turns it into a lead machine.
DIY Steps Optimise your LinkedIn profile for inbound leads ▼
  1. Headline: Do not put "Zinzino Partner" or "Network Marketer." Try: "Helping people discover their Omega-6:3 ratio and what to do about it | Health Advocate | Zinzino NZ" or simply "Is your Omega ratio off? Most people don't know. I can show you how to find out."
  2. Banner image: Create a simple Canva banner. Dark background, your most important number stat (e.g. "1 in 97% of people have an Omega imbalance"), your website URL. Use Zinzino's brand colours if you want.
  3. About section: Start with a question, not a bio. "Did you know that 97% of people in Western countries have an Omega-6:3 imbalance, and most have never been tested?" Then your story. Then what you do. End with a soft CTA: "Curious? Find your number at themostimportantnumber.com"
  4. Featured section: Link to themostimportantnumber.com and your Zinzino store link as featured items with a custom thumbnail and clear label.
  5. Experience: Write your Zinzino role like a professional: "Health Advocate & Partner | Zinzino NZ" with a description focused on the product science, not the earnings claim.
P LinkedIn content that actually gets engagement
  • ✓Post 3 to 4 times per week. Not every day, not once a week.
  • ✓First line of every post must be a scroll-stopper (question or bold claim)
  • ✓Use the "3-2-1 rule": 3 value posts, 2 story posts, 1 soft offer per week
  • ✓Always end posts with a question to drive comments (LinkedIn rewards comment activity)
  • ✓Comment on 10 posts per day in your target audience's network. Not "Great post!" Real thoughts.

"Most people don't know their Omega-6:3 ratio. Here's why it matters more than your cholesterol..."

Sanity check: After 2 weeks of posting, look at your profile views. If they are not increasing week-on-week, your headlines are not stopping scrollers. Go back to step one in the DIY section and rewrite your headline.

Facebook: The Slow Burn Engine
Warm your existing network and build a discoverable community
Pending
Facebook is not dead. For your demographic (40 to 65 year olds with disposable income and health concerns), it is the primary social platform. Your existing Facebook profile (facebook.com/john.macdougall.967) is an asset you have not fully activated. Facebook also gives you a free tool that LinkedIn does not: groups. A well-run health group is a free lead generation engine that runs 24 hours a day.
P Facebook profile as a landing page
  • ✓Profile photo: Professional, clear face shot. Not a logo, not sunglasses.
  • ✓Cover photo: Add a Canva graphic with your positioning statement and themostimportantnumber.com
  • ✓Bio section: Add your website link and a one-line hook. "Helping New Zealanders discover their most important health number."
  • ✓Pin a post to the top of your profile that introduces your story and links to your landing page
DIY Steps Create and run a free Facebook health group ▼
  1. Go to facebook.com/groups/create. Name it something topic-first, not brand-first. Good examples: "Omega Balance NZ," "The 3:1 Club," "Test Don't Guess | NZ Health Community." Bad examples: "John's Zinzino Group," "Team Macdougall Health."
  2. Set group to Public. This is important for discoverability. You want people searching health topics to land here.
  3. Write a group description that leads with the problem (most people have an Omega imbalance and have no idea) and positions the group as a free resource, not a sales channel.
  4. Post content 5 times per week: 2 educational posts, 2 personal story or member story posts, 1 engagement post (poll, question). Never more than 1 soft product mention per week.
  5. Invite your current contacts. Message them personally: "I've started a health community about Omega ratios. No selling, just real info. Would love to have you in there."
  6. After 30 days, any member who engages actively is a warm lead. At that point you can start a personal DM conversation (see Section 07).
Facebook group mistake to avoid: Do not post Zinzino product links in the group until members are warmed up. A group that looks like a shop front will not grow. A group that looks like a community will. Product comes after trust.
C Personal profile posting rhythm
  • ✓Post 4 to 5 times per week on your personal profile (not only the group)
  • ✓Mix: personal life posts, health education, results posts, behind-the-scenes of your wellness routine
  • ✓Share group posts from your personal profile to bridge audiences
  • ✓Use Facebook Stories daily. Even a 10-second clip of you taking your BalanceOil counts.

Short-Form Video (Reels & TikTok)
The fastest way to reach cold audiences without spending a dollar
Pending
You don't need to dance. You don't need a ring light. You need a phone, good lighting (face a window), and something interesting to say in the first 3 seconds. Short-form video is the only organic channel that can reach 10,000 strangers without you having any existing audience. One good Reel can generate 20 to 50 inbound inquiries. That's your whole week's lead target from a single 60-second video.
V Video formats that work for health products
  • ✓The reveal: "I got my blood test back. Here's what it said." (Show the result, explain it simply)
  • ✓The myth-bust: "Your doctor isn't checking this number. Here's why it matters."
  • ✓The before/after: "I had a 14:1 Omega ratio. 120 days later it was 3.8:1. Here's what changed."
  • ✓The explainer: "What is an Omega-6:3 ratio and why does it matter? (Under 60 seconds)"
  • ✓The day-in-the-life: Show your daily routine including taking your BalanceOil. No pitch. Just normal life.
DIY Steps Film and post your first Reel this week ▼
  1. Choose the "reveal" format for your first video. It is the easiest and most viewed format in health niches. Script: "I just got my Omega blood test back. [Pause] Most doctors don't order this test. [Show result on phone or card] My ratio was [X]. The optimal is 3:1. Here's why that matters and what I did about it."
  2. Film in natural light. Face a window. Phone in portrait mode. Don't over-edit. Raw and real outperforms polished and scripted in this niche.
  3. In the caption: explain the test, add 3 to 5 hashtags (#omegahealth #bloodtest #zinzino #healthnz #balanceoil), and end with "DM me 'test' if you want to know your number."
  4. Post to Instagram Reels, Facebook Reels, and TikTok (if you have or want an account) simultaneously. You shoot once and distribute everywhere.
  5. Reply to every comment in the first hour. The algorithm rewards early engagement heavily.
  6. If the video gets more than 500 views, repeat the same format with a different hook. You've found something that works.
You do not need TikTok to succeed with video. Facebook Reels reach is currently strong for 40+ audiences in NZ and AU. Instagram Reels are solid for 30 to 50. Focus on Facebook Reels first since your existing audience is already there.
Sanity check: If you post 5 Reels and none break 200 views, the problem is the hook (first 3 seconds), not the content. Try 3 completely different first sentences on the same video concept before deciding the format doesn't work.

Content Calendar & AI System
Stop winging it. Build a 30-day system you can repeat on autopilot.
Pending
The number one reason organic social fails for network marketers is inconsistency. You post 5 times in one week and then nothing for two weeks. The algorithm buries you. W Your weekly content rhythm
Monday

Education post: science fact about omega health. LinkedIn + Facebook.

Tuesday

Facebook Story or Reel: quick lifestyle clip. 15 to 30 seconds. No pitch.

Wednesday

Personal story or result post. Your journey or a customer's (with permission). LinkedIn + Facebook.

Thursday

Engagement post: question, poll, or myth vs. fact. Facebook Group priority.

Friday

Soft offer post: "I'm sending 3 free test kits this month. DM me 'test' to claim one."

Weekend

Optional Reel or Story. Behind the scenes of your health routine. 10 minutes max effort.

DIY Steps Set up your AI content pipeline in under 30 minutes ▼
  1. Go to claude.ai (free account is fine). Create a new conversation called "John Content System."
  2. Paste this system prompt at the start: "You are a content assistant for John Macdougall, a Zinzino health advocate based in New Zealand. He promotes science-backed omega supplements and the BalanceTest. His audience is NZ professionals aged 35-60. His tone is direct, curious, and experienced. He never uses MLM language. He always leads with health education, never with the business opportunity. Keep posts under 200 words. No em dashes. No corporate speak."
  3. Each Monday morning, ask Claude: "Write this week's 5 posts following the Monday to Friday schedule: Monday education, Tuesday story hook, Wednesday personal result, Thursday engagement question, Friday soft offer."
  4. Edit each post so it sounds like you (add your real numbers, your actual story, your voice). Claude gives you the structure. You give it the truth.
  5. Use a free scheduler like Buffer (free tier) to schedule all 5 posts on Monday morning. Then do not think about content again until next Monday.
Time investment reality: With this system, your content creation time is 45 to 60 minutes every Monday morning. That's it. The rest of your social media time goes to engagement (comments, DMs, replies), which is where leads actually come from.

DM Strategy: Zero Spam, High Conversion
The right way to convert warm followers into paying customers
Pending
DMs are where leads become customers. But most network marketers get this completely backwards: they DM first and warm up second. The system in this playbook reverses that. You warm up through public content (sections 2 to 6) and only DM people who have shown genuine interest. That one change eliminates rejection, eliminates the "spam" feeling, and dramatically increases your conversion rate.
Never do this: Do not DM someone you have not interacted with publicly and open with a product pitch or "I thought you might be interested in an opportunity." This is the behaviour that makes people cringe at MLM. One person posting about it publicly can tank your credibility.
T The three triggers for a first DM
  • ✓They commented on one of your posts with a question about the product or test
  • ✓They liked or reacted to 3 or more of your posts in the past 2 weeks
  • ✓They sent you a DM first (always respond immediately, within 2 hours ideally)
DIY Steps The 3-message DM sequence that converts without pressure ▼
  1. Message 1 (open with curiosity, not pitch): "Hey [name], noticed you've been engaging with my posts about Omega health. Do you currently take any supplements, or is this something new for you?"
  2. Message 2 (after they reply, reflect their situation back): "Got it. A lot of people in that situation have never had their Omega ratio tested. The interesting thing is it's a simple home test. Want me to send you the link to find out your number? No pressure either way."
  3. Message 3 (after they express interest): "Here you go: [themostimportantnumber.com link]. If you do decide to test, the result comes back in a few weeks and I can help you understand what it means and what to do about it. Any questions, just ask."
  4. After they purchase or test: follow up once at 2 weeks to check in. After they retest and improve: ask if they know anyone else who might want to find their number. This is your referral loop.
  5. Track every DM conversation in a simple spreadsheet: name, platform, last message, status (cold, warm, customer, partner). Review every Friday and follow up any conversations that have gone quiet for more than 7 days with a light touch: "Hey, just checking if you had any questions about the test."
C Common objections and how to handle them
  • ✓"Is this MLM?" "It's a referral model, same as how most supplement brands work online. I recommend it because it worked for me." Don't apologise for it.
  • ✓"I already take fish oil." "Most fish oil supplements don't move the ratio needle, which is the whole point. The test will show you if yours is actually working."
  • ✓"Seems expensive." "The test is $X. If it shows everything is fine, great. If not, you have data to act on. Most people find it's worth knowing."
  • ✓No reply after 2 messages. Move on. One light follow-up after 2 weeks maximum. Never chase.

Recruit Without Sounding Desperate
Attract partners by being the person they want to work with
Pending
Recruiting in network marketing fails for one reason: people recruit as if they need the other person more than the other person needs the opportunity. Flip that. You are not recruiting. You are sharing that a spot on a serious team exists, and you are selective about who joins. Scarcity and standards are more attractive than openness and desperation. This is basic human psychology and it works every time.
R Who to recruit and who not to
  • ✓Recruit: People who bought the product and got results. They already believe. The conversation is easy.
  • ✓Recruit: People who follow you because they respect your expertise in health. They already trust you.
  • ✓Recruit: People who have specifically asked about the business model or how you got started.
  • ✓Do not recruit: People who are skeptical of the product. Convincing them of the product AND the opportunity doubles the resistance.
  • ✓Do not recruit: People who are attracted by the income claims, not the product. They will not build a real business.
DIY Steps The "I've been building something" conversation ▼
  1. Never open a partner conversation with the income opportunity. Open with the product conversation first. Always.
  2. After someone has been a customer for at least 30 days and mentions positive results, use this transition: "I'm glad you're feeling the difference. Can I ask, do you know anyone else in a similar situation who might want to find their number?" If they say yes and seem enthusiastic, add: "Honestly, some of my best customers have ended up referring a few people and earning back more than they spend on their own products."
  3. If they express interest in the business side: "I'm pretty selective about who I work with because I want to actually help people build this properly. What does your situation look like right now?" This one question filters serious people from casual ones.
  4. Share your Zinzino partner registration link only after this filter conversation. Not before.
  5. Create one post per month on LinkedIn and Facebook that tells the story of what you are building, without an income claim, framed around the mission: "I've helped [X] people in NZ find their Omega number this year. A few of those have started doing the same for others in their network. If that's something you've thought about, I'm happy to have a conversation."
Income claims on social media: In NZ, you cannot make specific income claims for MLM opportunities without proper disclosure. Never say "earn $X per month" or "replace your income." Say "earn back what you spend on your own products" or "I cover my health costs through referrals." Stay compliant.
