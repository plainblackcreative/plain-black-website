01
Google Business Profile
The free foundation that drives local calls
Free Sample
🎯
Goal: A fully optimized Google Business Profile that puts you in the map pack for high-intent local searches. This is free traffic you are currently leaving on the table every single day.
What to fix first
  • Business name exactly matches your website
  • Primary category set to your specific trade
  • Service area matches where you actually work
  • Hours correct, including emergency availability
  • At least 10 photos: trucks, team, before and after
What moves the ranking needle
  • Review velocity: 1 to 2 new reviews per week
  • Responding to every review within 24 hours
  • Weekly posts showing recent jobs in your city
  • Services list complete with specific job types
  • Q&A section with your own answered questions
Common mistake: Setting your service area too narrow. Add every city you can realistically reach in under 45 minutes. Google shows you to searchers based on your service area, not just your business address.
DIY StepsHow to set up your service area correctly
▼
1
Go to business.google.com and select your profile. Click "Edit profile" on the left panel.
2
Click "Business location." If you operate service-area only with no storefront, turn off the physical address toggle. Then click "Service area."
3
Add your primary city plus every surrounding city you serve. Do not add areas you cannot reach in under 45 minutes. More is not better if you cannot realistically serve those areas.
4
Click Save. Google may re-verify your profile. Check your email for verification steps within 48 hours.
Sanity check: Search your trade type and city on Google Maps on your phone. If your business appears in the map pack, this is working. If it does not appear after 2 weeks with a complete profile, check your verification status at business.google.com.

02
Local Service Ads Setup
Get the Google Guaranteed badge and pay per lead, not per click
Free Sample
🎯
Goal: Get your Google Guaranteed badge and appear in LSA results above regular search ads. LSAs charge per qualified lead, not per click, and the badge significantly increases call rates for trades businesses.
What LSAs give you
  • Google Guaranteed badge next to your name
  • Appears above regular paid search results
  • Pay per qualified lead, not per click
  • Dispute bad leads for credits
  • Directly tied to your Google Business Profile
What you need to apply
  • Valid contractor's license for your trade
  • Current business insurance certificate
  • Background check for owner and employees
  • Business registration documents
  • Verified Google Business Profile (Section 1)
This takes time. Plan for it. The LSA verification process typically takes 1 to 3 weeks. Submit your documents as soon as Section 1 is complete. Do not wait until you are ready to run ads. The clock starts when you apply, not when you decide to.
DIY StepsHow to apply for Google Local Service Ads
▼
1
Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads. Click "Get started" and sign in with the same Google account as your Google Business Profile.
2
Select your business category and service area. Be precise. LSA results are location-specific and your service area here must match your Google Business Profile.
3
Upload your documents. You will need your license certificate, insurance certificate, and business registration as PDFs. Have these ready before you start.
4
Complete the background check process for yourself and any employees who interact with customers. This is done through a third-party provider linked from the LSA dashboard.
5
Set your weekly budget. Start at $200 to $400 per week depending on your trade and market. Set it too low and Google will not show your listing enough to generate leads.
Sanity check: After submitting, your LSA dashboard should show "In review" status. If it stays on "Action required" for more than 3 days, log in and check which document is flagged. Expired insurance certificates are the most common blocker.
