Responding to Negative Reviews Without Making It Worse
The weekend wait-time complaints. The cold coffee. The rude staff accusation. Handled.
ChatGPT · Free
Pending
Goal: Give Jamie a word-for-word structure for every negative review at Swell & Co. so the response is calm, human, and turns every potential customer who reads it into a supporter.

The four-part structure for Swell & Co.

  • 1. Name the specific complaint. Don't be vague.
  • 2. Apologise without excuses. No "but it was busy."
  • 3. Invite them to follow up at hello@swellandco.com.au
  • 4. Keep it under 100 words. Confidence is brief.

Swell & Co. specific situations

  • Weekend wait time: "Saturday mornings are our busiest and we didn't manage the queue well enough. That's on us."
  • Cold coffee: "A cold flat white is never acceptable, regardless of how slammed we were. Please reach out directly."
  • Staff complaint: "That's not how we want anyone to feel at Swell. Reach out to Jamie directly at hello@swellandco.com.au."
Remember this: When a potential customer reads your response to a negative review, they are deciding whether to trust you. A calm, direct, accountable response wins them. A defensive one loses them permanently.

4

The Fake Review on Swell & Co.'s Profile and How to Fight It
What to do with the review you know isn't from a real customer.
Google Business Profile · Free
Pending
Goal: Give Jamie a clear process for identifying, reporting, and publicly responding to the fake review currently on Swell & Co.'s profile, and for protecting the profile against future attacks.

Signs the suspicious review isn't legitimate

  • Reviewer profile has no photo and very few or no other reviews
  • Review references things that didn't happen or don't match Swell's setup
  • Language reads like it was written by someone who never visited Burleigh
  • Account was created recently with no activity history
  • Arrived suspiciously close to when Alfred or another competitor opened nearby

What Google will remove

  • Reviews that violate Google's content policy (spam, conflict of interest, off-topic)
  • Reviews from someone with no record of visiting Swell & Co.
  • Coordinated fake attacks with documentation provided
  • Reviews containing false factual claims
  • Google will NOT remove reviews just because they're negative and unwelcome
How to respond publicly to the suspicious review: "We don't have any record of a visit matching this description. We'd genuinely love the opportunity to make it right if this was a real experience at Swell. Please reach out to Jamie directly at hello@swellandco.com.au." Calm. Professional. Anyone reading it can see the story.
Step-by-step: Flag the suspicious review for removal ▼
1
Go to Swell & Co.'s Google Maps listing. Find the review in question.
2
Click the three dots (...) next to the review. Select "Report review."
3
Select the most accurate reason: "Conflict of interest" if it looks like a competitor, or "Spam" if it looks like a coordinated attack.
4
If not removed within a week, go to business.google.com and use the "Request review removal" tool under Reviews.
5
If still not resolved, document everything: screenshots of the review, the reviewer's profile, timestamps, and any evidence from that date's service records. Escalate to the Google Business Profile Help Community.
