Before any marketing runs, you need your revenue model locked. $100K per year is around $8,334 per month. The fastest path is a blend of recurring sessions and small group packages, not chasing one-off drop-ins. This section sets the financial floor everything else builds on.

Lock Your Core Offer Stack
  • Define your entry offer: 4-session intro pack ($120–$160) to reduce first-purchase friction
  • Create a monthly recurring package ($395–$595/mo) for 2x or 3x per week clients
  • Price a small-group session option (2–4 people, same time slot, shared cost = higher revenue per hour)
  • Set a clear limit on total active clients so you protect your schedule and energy
  • Write your offer as one sentence: "I help [who] achieve [result] in [timeframe] without [barrier]"
Revenue Mix Model (Fill This In)
  • Ongoing 1:1 clients × monthly rate = $____ (your stable base)
  • Small group slots × weekly sessions × rate = $____ (your growth lever)
  • Intro packs × monthly conversions = $____ (your acquisition funnel)
  • Add the three numbers. If it doesn't hit $8,334, adjust slot count, not price
Sanity Check If you're not at 60% of target after 90 days, the problem is almost always client acquisition, not pricing. Don't cut prices first. Fix the front end.

Target Audience — The Gym-Fringe Client
Pending
Turtle Shell Fitness wins by going where big gyms can't follow. Your market is people who have tried gyms and bounced, people intimidated by conventional gym culture, older adults wanting function over aesthetics, people recovering from injury or managing chronic conditions, and anyone who feels like fitness spaces weren't built for them. This is a large, underserved, and fiercely loyal audience when you get it right.
Define Your Primary Client Avatar
  • Write one paragraph describing your best current (or ideal) client in specific detail — age, lifestyle, why gyms failed them, what they actually want
  • Identify their #1 fear (being judged, getting hurt again, wasting money, not seeing results)
  • Identify their #1 desire (keeping up with grandkids, not needing a knee replacement, having energy again, just moving without pain)
  • Note where they spend time online and what they read — this drives your content and ad targeting
Audience Segments to Consider
  • Adults 45+ focused on longevity, mobility, and staying independent (high LTV, low churn)
  • Post-injury or post-surgery clients returning to movement (referred by physios and GPs)
  • Body-neutral / anti-diet-culture clients who want health without the gym aesthetic pressure
  • Beginners who've never exercised seriously and need a non-intimidating entry point
  • Neurodiverse individuals or those with anxiety who need predictable, low-stimulation environments
Why This Market Wins Gym-fringe clients have a high emotional reason to commit once they find something that works for them. They don't price-shop the way gym membership browsers do. Retention is significantly higher because their alternative is "nothing" not "a cheaper gym."

Website, SEO & Digital Presence Audit
Pending
turtleshellfitness.com is your 24/7 salesperson. If someone lands on it and can't immediately understand what you do, who you help, and how to take the next step, you're losing clients before they ever contact you. This section covers what to audit, what to fix first, and how to get found locally without paying for ads.
Website Audit Checklist (Do This First)
  • Homepage: within 5 seconds a stranger can tell who you help and what you offer — test this with someone who doesn't know you
  • Single clear call to action above the fold (one button, one link — not three options)
  • Mobile speed test: go to pagespeed.web.dev — score should be 70+ on mobile
  • Contact details visible without scrolling (phone or booking link in header)
  • At least one real photo of you (not stock) — trust collapses without a face
Local SEO Foundations (Free to Do)
  • Google Business Profile: claim and fully complete at business.google.com — set hours, services, photos, description
  • Website title tag includes: your city + "personal trainer" (e.g. "Personal Trainer Devonport | Turtle Shell Fitness")
  • Create one page per major service with the service name + location in the heading (e.g. "Small Group Fitness Training Devonport Tasmania")
  • Add your business to True Local, Hotfrog, Yelp AU, and Yellow Pages AU — takes 20 mins, improves local search signals
  • Ask every current client to leave a Google review. 10+ reviews with 4.5+ stars is the minimum bar to show up in local 3-pack
Common Mistake Having a booking link that goes to a generic Calendly with no context. Your booking page should say what happens at the first session, how long it takes, and what it costs. Reduce friction at every step.

META Ads Strategy — Reaching Gym-Fringe Audiences
Pending
META (Facebook and Instagram) is the right platform for Turtle Shell Fitness because your audience skews 35–65 and makes purchase decisions based on trust and emotion, not impulse. Ads here build familiarity before someone ever clicks. Budget does not need to be large. A consistent $10–$20 NZD per day with the right creative will generate leads.
Before You Run Any Ads
  • META Business Account set up at business.facebook.com
  • META Pixel installed on turtleshellfitness.com (set up in Events Manager — takes 15 mins with the browser plugin)
  • Facebook Page with complete profile, profile photo, and at least 5 recent posts
  • A dedicated landing page on your website for ad traffic (not your homepage — a single focused page with one CTA)
Campaign Setup (Start Simple)
  • Objective: Leads (use the built-in Lead Form to reduce friction — don't send people to your website cold)
  • Audience: Devonport and surrounds, radius 20–30km (covers Burnie, Ulverstone, Latrobe), ages 38–65, interests: fitness, wellness, yoga, walking, physiotherapy, healthy ageing
  • Budget: Start at $10 AUD/day. Do not scale before you have 3+ leads at a cost you can afford
  • Ad format: Single image or short video (15–30 sec). Your face and voice convert better than polished stock creative
  • Run for 7 days minimum before judging results. Do not change targeting mid-flight
What Works for This Audience Ads that acknowledge the problem directly — "Tired of feeling out of place at the gym?" — outperform generic "Get Fit" ads. Your audience has been burnt before. Show them you understand that.
DIY
How to Film a 20-Second Ad on Your Phone That Converts
▼
  1. Stand somewhere clean and well-lit (natural light from a window, not overhead fluorescent)
  2. First 3 seconds: say the problem out loud. "Most gyms aren't built for people over 50."
  3. Seconds 4–12: say who you help and what you do differently. "At Turtle Shell, we train around your body, not against it."
  4. Seconds 13–18: give the outcome. "My clients feel stronger, move better, and actually enjoy training."
  5. Last 2 seconds: clear CTA. "Click below to book a free 15-minute chat."
  6. No fancy editing needed. Raw, honest, direct to camera always wins against polished studio reels for this demographic

Google Ads & Local Search Intent Capture
Pending
Google Search captures people who are actively looking. Someone searching "personal trainer Wellington for beginners" or "gentle fitness classes near me" is ready to buy. This is different from META where you interrupt scrolling. Google is expensive per click, so it works best once you have a high-converting landing page and know your close rate.
Start With Organic (Free) Before Paying
  • Optimise your Google Business Profile first (Section 3) — this gets you into local map results for free
  • Create a blog post targeting "personal trainer for beginners [your city]" — 600 words, answer the questions your audience searches
  • Add a FAQ section to your website answering: "Is this right for me if I've never trained before?" and "What happens at the first session?"
Google Ads (When You're Ready)
  • Start with Search campaigns only (not Display or Performance Max)
  • Target high-intent keywords: "personal trainer Devonport", "personal trainer Tasmania", "fitness classes for beginners Devonport", "personal trainer for older adults north west Tasmania"
  • Use exact match and phrase match only. Broad match burns budget on irrelevant searches
  • Budget: $15–25 AUD/day to start. Review search term reports weekly and add negatives (e.g. "free", "jobs", "certification")
  • Landing page for ad traffic must match the ad headline exactly. Different message = wasted click
Do Not Run Google Ads before your website converts. If your site doesn't clearly answer "what do I do next," paying for clicks is just paying for bounces. Fix the site first, then turn on ads.

Content Strategy — Building Trust Before the Sale
Pending
Content for Turtle Shell Fitness is not about posting daily and going viral. It is about showing up consistently as the person who understands clients that everyone else overlooks. One or two quality posts per week, consistently, over 6 months, will build more trust than any ad campaign.
Content Pillars (Rotate Through These)
  • Pillar 1 — Validation: "You're not broken, the gym just wasn't built for you" — posts that acknowledge your client's experience
  • Pillar 2 — Education: simple movement tips, myth-busting, "what actually helps over 50" type content
  • Pillar 3 — Proof: client wins (with permission), transformation stories focused on energy and function not weight loss aesthetics
  • Pillar 4 — Behind the scenes: how you train clients, your approach, what makes Turtle Shell different
  • Pillar 5 — CTA: 1 in 5 posts can make a direct offer or invite people to inquire
Platforms to Prioritise
  • Facebook: primary platform for 40–65 demographic, share to local Devonport community groups and north west Tasmania groups
  • Instagram: secondary — use Reels for reach, carousel posts for saves and shares
  • Google Business Profile: post weekly (it boosts local SEO and appears in search results)
  • Email list: if you have one, a fortnightly email beats any social post for conversion

KPIs, Tracking & Lead Handling Automation
Pending
You can't improve what you don't measure. This section covers the 5 numbers that matter for Turtle Shell Fitness, how to track them without a fancy dashboard, and how to automate your lead response so nothing falls through the cracks.
The 5 Numbers That Matter
  • Cost per lead (CPL): total ad spend ÷ number of leads. Target under $30 NZD
  • Lead-to-consultation rate: how many leads book a call or session. Target 40%+
  • Consultation-to-client rate: how many consults convert to paying clients. Target 60%+
  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR): track weekly in your Google Sheet (Section 1)
  • Client retention rate: what % of clients are still active after 3 months. Target 70%+
Lead Handling — The 2-Hour Rule
  • Every lead gets a personal response within 2 hours. Speed to respond is the #1 conversion factor for small fitness businesses
  • First message: "Hey [name], thanks for reaching out to Turtle Shell Fitness. Quick question — what's the main thing you're looking to work on?" — one open question starts the conversation
  • Set a phone notification for META Lead Form submissions so you never miss one
  • After 3 exchanges, offer a 15-min call or a free first session — don't stay in messenger forever
Simple Automation (Free with Make.com)
  • Connect META Lead Ads to Gmail via Make.com (free tier) — every new lead triggers an email to you instantly
  • Set up a Calendly booking link that sends an automatic confirmation email with what to bring and what to expect
  • Add a Calendly link to your META lead form as a thank-you redirect — some leads will self-book without you needing to chase
High Risk Area Automation setup is where most people stall. If Make.com feels overwhelming, skip it for now. Manual response within 2 hours beats a broken automation every time. Come back to automation once your lead volume justifies it (10+ leads per week).
