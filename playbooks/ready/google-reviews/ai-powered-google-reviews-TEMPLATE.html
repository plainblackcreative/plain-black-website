Handling Fake and Malicious Reviews on [[BUSINESS_NAME]]'s Profile
What to do with the review you know isn't from a real customer.
Google Business Profile · Free
Pending
Goal: Give [[OWNER_NAME]] a clear process for identifying, reporting, and publicly responding to suspicious reviews on [[BUSINESS_NAME]]'s profile, and for protecting the profile against future attacks.

Signs a review may not be legitimate

  • Reviewer profile has no photo and very few or no other reviews
  • Review references things that didn't happen or don't match [[BUSINESS_NAME]]'s setup
  • Language reads like it was written by someone who never visited [[CITY]]
  • Account was created recently with no activity history
  • Arrived suspiciously close to when a competitor opened or pushed a promotion

What Google will remove

  • Reviews that violate Google's content policy (spam, conflict of interest, off-topic)
  • Reviews from someone with no record of visiting [[BUSINESS_NAME]]
  • Coordinated fake attacks with documentation provided
  • Reviews containing false factual claims
  • Google will NOT remove reviews just because they're negative and unwelcome
How to respond publicly to a suspicious review: "We don't have any record of a visit matching this description. We'd genuinely love the opportunity to make it right if this was a real experience at [[BUSINESS_NAME]]. Please reach out to [[OWNER_NAME]] directly at [[OWNER_EMAIL]]." Calm. Professional. Anyone reading it can see the story.
Step-by-step: Flag a suspicious review for removal ▼
1
Go to [[BUSINESS_NAME]]'s Google Maps listing. Find the review in question.
2
Click the three dots (…) next to the review. Select "Report review."
3
Select the most accurate reason: "Conflict of interest" if it looks like a competitor, "Spam" if it looks like a coordinated attack, or "Off-topic" if it's clearly not about your business.
4
If not removed within a week, go to business.google.com and use the "Request review removal" tool under Reviews.
5
If still not resolved, document everything: screenshots of the review, the reviewer's profile, timestamps, and any evidence from that date. Escalate to the Google Business Profile Help Community.
