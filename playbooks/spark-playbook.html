What's wrong with the current email

Rachel sends the same bulk deal list to every business customer, every month. Here's why that's leaving money on the table.

1

No segmentation

Every customer gets the same iPhone and Samsung list, whether they're a sole trader with one phone or a fleet of 20 devices. A plumber who just bought an iPhone 17e last month still gets the iPhone 17e deal. This creates noise and trains recipients to ignore the email entirely.

2

No call to action

The email lists deals but never tells the reader what to do next. No "Reply to this email," no booking link, no urgency driver beyond a fine-print expiry date buried at the bottom. The reader has to figure out the next step themselves.

3

No tracking or follow-up

Rachel has no idea who opened, who clicked, who's interested. She can't prioritize her follow-up calls. Every lead is equally cold. She's making outbound calls blind.

4

No upsell pathway

Accessories are a footnote. There's no cross-sell into plans, broadband, cloud, or other Spark Business products. Each email is a dead end rather than an entry point into a broader relationship.

5

Zero personalisation

The greeting is "Hello!" with no name, no company reference, no mention of their current devices or plan. It reads like a flyer, not a message from their account manager.

The AI-Driven Funnel

Five automated stages that turn Rachel's contact list into a self-qualifying pipeline. AI handles the grunt work; Rachel closes the warm leads.

1
Trigger: Contract approaching 18+ months

Smart Segmentation & Enrichment

AI scans the customer database and segments contacts by device age, plan type, fleet size, and spend tier. Each contact gets a profile: "3-device fleet, iPhone 15, Pro plan, 22 months in." This segmentation drives everything downstream.

AI: auto-segment CRM integration
2
Trigger: Segment assigned

Personalised Outreach Sequence

AI generates a tailored email for each segment (not each person, to keep it manageable). The email highlights the one or two most relevant deals, calculates their specific savings, and includes a booking link. A follow-up SMS goes out 48 hours later to non-openers.

AI: draft emails AI: SMS follow-up Email platform
3
Trigger: Email opened / link clicked / booking made

Intent Scoring & Prioritisation

Every interaction is scored. Opened email = 1 point. Clicked deal link = 3 points. Visited Spark phone page = 5 points. Booked a call = 10 points. Rachel gets a daily "Hot Leads" list ranked by score, so she calls the people most likely to buy first.

AI: lead scoring Rachel: call hot leads
4
Trigger: Deal closed or call completed

Automated Upsell & Cross-sell

Once a device deal closes, AI triggers a follow-up sequence: accessory bundle offer (day 1), broadband/cloud cross-sell (day 7), plan optimisation review (day 30). Each touchpoint is relevant to what they just bought.

AI: upsell sequences AI: product matching
5
Trigger: Ongoing / quarterly

Retention & Re-engagement

Customers who didn't bite get a "nurture" drip: quarterly check-ins, relevant Spark Business content, and a re-engagement offer 6 months before their next contract end. No lead is ever truly dead, just not ready yet.

AI: nurture drip AI: re-engagement Rachel: quarterly review

Recommended Tech Stack

Pragmatic tools that work for a regional SME client lead. No enterprise bloat. Everything integrates and can be stood up in under two weeks.

Email & Automation

Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign. Handles segmented sends, open/click tracking, automated sequences, and booking link integration.

~$50-150/mo depending on list size

CRM / Pipeline

HubSpot Free or Pipedrive. Tracks every contact, their devices, contract dates, and interaction history. Feeds the AI segmentation layer.

Free to $30/mo

AI Content Layer

Claude API via a simple internal tool or n8n workflow. Takes segment data and generates personalised email drafts, SMS copy, and upsell sequences. Rachel reviews and approves.

~$20-50/mo API costs

Booking

Calendly or Cal.com. Embedded in every email. Lets customers self-schedule 10-minute upgrade calls. Syncs to Rachel's calendar automatically.

Free to $12/mo

SMS Follow-up

Twilio or a local NZ provider like Burst SMS. Sends a short follow-up text to non-openers 48 hours after email. "Hey [Name], did you see the upgrade deal I sent? Happy to chat: [booking link]"

~$0.08/SMS

Workflow Orchestration

n8n (self-hosted) or Make.com. Connects the CRM, email platform, AI layer, and SMS into automated workflows. The glue that makes the funnel hands-free.

Free (self-hosted) to $30/mo

All-in platform costs. The system pays for itself if it closes one extra device deal per month.
