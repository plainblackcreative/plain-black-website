Audit What They Control

Before you pull the plug, you need to know exactly what Ordermeal has its hands on. Some of it is yours. Some of it might be theirs. This section tells you exactly what to check and what it means if they own it.

What to Check Do This First

  • Log into your domain registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.) and confirm YOUR email is the account owner, not an Ordermeal address
  • Check Google Business Profile: search "manage my business" on Google. Is it linked to your email or an Ordermeal one?
  • Log into your Ordermeal dashboard. Screenshot every screen: what data, reviews, and customer info is stored there
  • Check if your Facebook/Instagram business account is connected to an Ordermeal email or app
  • Check your website URL. Can you access the hosting directly? Or does it only exist through Ordermeal's platform?
  • Note your current commission rate and the last 4 weeks of online order volume from Ordermeal's dashboard
Red flag: If any account (domain, Google, Facebook, website hosting) has an email address you don't recognise or control, that account is at risk. Do not cancel Ordermeal or make any changes until you have regained access to those accounts.
1
How to find out who owns your domain
+
  1. Go to who.is or domaintools.com
  2. Type in your domain name (e.g. yourpizzeria.co.nz)
  3. Look at the "Registrant Email" field. That email address owns the domain.
  4. If it's an Ordermeal or unknown email, you do not control your domain.
  5. Note it down. You'll deal with this in Section 2.
Stop check: If the registrant email is @ordermeal.co.nz or similar, do not cancel your service yet. Cancelling first could result in losing the domain. Follow Section 2 first.
2
How to check your Google Business ownership
+
  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in with your business Google account
  2. If you see your business listed, you have access. Good.
  3. Click "Users" or "Manage Permissions." Check who is listed as Owner vs Manager.
  4. If Ordermeal or an unfamiliar email is listed as Owner (not just Manager), flag it for Section 3.
  5. If you can't log in at all, your business Google account may be controlled by Ordermeal entirely.
There are two roles: Owner and Manager. Owners can remove others. Managers can't. You need to be Owner.

Reclaim Your Domain

Your domain name is your identity online. If someone else owns it, they own your business address. This section walks you through three scenarios: you already own it, they own it and will transfer, they own it and are difficult.
Scenario A (easy): You control the domain. Just make sure your hosting is independent. Skip to Section 4.
Scenario B (work required): They hold the domain but are cooperative. Follow the DIY steps below.
Scenario C (get help): They refuse to transfer or go quiet. This is a dispute and may require your domain registrar's intervention or a new domain.
1
Request a domain transfer from Ordermeal
+
  1. First, register an account with a reputable NZ domain registrar if you don't have one. Recommended: Cloudflare Registrar (free transfers), Namecheap, or Metaname (NZ-based)
  2. Email or message Ordermeal support. Subject line: "Domain Transfer Request for [yourdomain.co.nz]"
  3. State: "I am the business owner and require the transfer of [domain] to my personal registrar account. Please provide the transfer authorisation code (EPP code)."
  4. If they comply, use the EPP code in your new registrar to initiate the transfer. Transfers take 5 to 7 days for .co.nz.
  5. Do NOT cancel your Ordermeal service until the transfer is confirmed complete.
Stop check: If you don't hear back within 3 business days, escalate to Ordermeal's management. If no response, see Scenario C below.
2
Scenario C: They won't transfer your domain
+
  1. Contact the domain registrar directly (find them via WHOIS lookup). File a registrant change request or dispute.
  2. For .co.nz domains, contact InternetNZ (the .nz registry). They handle domain disputes in New Zealand.
  3. As a parallel action, register a close variant of your domain (e.g. yourpizzerianz.co.nz) as a backup.
  4. Start telling customers your new domain via in-store signage, social, and Google. Begin redirecting traffic.
  5. Document all communication with Ordermeal for any future dispute.
Most NZ hosting disputes are resolved quickly once you contact InternetNZ directly. Ordermeal is not above the law here.

Common Mistakes

  • Cancelling Ordermeal before securing the domain transfer
  • Accepting "we'll transfer it later" without a written timeline
  • Assuming the domain is yours because you named it
  • Not keeping copies of all communication with Ordermeal

Own Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile drives local foot traffic, phone calls, and online orders. If Ordermeal owns it, they can edit your hours, remove your photos, and even list a competitor. Here's how to take it back and lock it down.
1
Claim or recover your Google Business Profile
+
  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in with a Google account you own
  2. Search for your business. If it shows up and says "Claim this business," claim it. Verify via phone or postcard.
  3. If the profile is already claimed by someone else, click "Request access." Google will contact the current owner.
  4. If the owner doesn't respond within 7 days, Google gives you the ability to take over via the business address verification.
  5. Once you have access, go to Users and remove any Ordermeal email addresses. Change the Owner to your own Google account.
Stop check: If you can't verify via phone or postcard because the registered phone/address is an Ordermeal one, you'll need to use Google's dispute process at support.google.com/business.

Profile Optimisation Checklist Do This Once You Have Access

  • Add a direct online ordering link pointing to YOUR website (not Ordermeal)
  • Upload 10 to 20 fresh, high-quality food photos (use your phone, good lighting, natural setting)
  • Confirm your hours are correct and match your actual trading times
  • Write a business description that includes your suburb, pizza style, and a clear CTA (e.g. "Order direct at yourwebsite.co.nz")
  • Enable Google Messaging so customers can contact you direct
  • Set up automated replies for common questions via Google's Q&A feature

Set Up Commission-Free Ordering

This is the core swap. Replace Ordermeal's ordering system with one you own, pay nothing per order, and keep 100% of every sale. The right tool depends on your situation. This section tells you exactly which one to use.

Which Tool to Use

  • Square Online (free tier) - Best if you want zero cost and a simple setup. Includes ordering, payment processing, and a basic menu page. Commission-free. You pay Square's standard card processing fee only (around 1.6% NZ).
  • Wix with Wix Restaurants - Best if you want a proper website with ordering built in. Free plan available, paid plans From $17 USD/month. No order commissions.
  • Squarespace with Acuity or third-party order form - Best if you want premium design and are comfortable with a small monthly cost ($16 USD). No commissions.
  • OrderMark or GloriaFood (now Zuppler) - Free online ordering plugins you can embed on any existing website. No commissions. Best for pizza-specific menus.
1
Set up Square Online ordering (fastest option)
+
  1. Go to squareup.com and create a free Square account using your business email
  2. Click "Online Store" from the dashboard. Choose "Ordering" as your store type.
  3. Add your menu items: name, description, price, and a photo for each pizza. Use your phone for photos.
  4. Set your pickup and delivery areas. Enter your address and set a delivery radius in km.
  5. Connect a bank account for payouts (BSB and account number). Funds land within 1 to 2 business days.
  6. Copy your Square Online store link (e.g. yourname.square.site). This is your new ordering URL.
  7. Add this link to your Google Business Profile, Instagram bio, and Facebook page immediately.
Stop check: Place a test order yourself before sharing the link. Confirm the order notification comes through and the menu looks correct on mobile.

Common Mistakes

  • Using a payment processor with hidden per-transaction fees that eat into savings
  • Not setting up delivery zone limits (you'll get orders from 40km away)
  • Keeping the Ordermeal link active on your Google profile while running both systems (confuses customers)
  • Not testing the mobile checkout experience before launch

Move Customers to Direct Orders

Setting up the ordering system is the easy part. The real work is getting your existing customers to use it instead of Ordermeal. Most will switch if you give them a reason. Here's exactly what to say and where to say it.

What to Post (Canva it in 10 minutes) Social + Google

  • Post: "We've got our own ordering now. Same great pizza, straight from us. Order at [your link]." No need to mention Ordermeal.
  • Add a tent card or sticker to every takeaway bag: "Order direct and get [10% off / free garlic bread / loyalty points]"
  • Update your Instagram and Facebook bio links to your direct ordering URL immediately
  • Pin a post to the top of your Facebook page with your new ordering link
  • Ask for the first 50 direct orders to leave a Google review. Simple exchange: direct order = please leave a review.
The offer: Give customers a single reason to order direct. A free drink, 10% off first order, or loyalty stamps. Mention it once. Don't over-explain it. "Order direct, get [X]" is enough.
1
Create your "order direct" social post in 10 minutes
+
  1. Open Canva.com (free). Search "Instagram post food."
  2. Pick a simple template. Change the text to: "[Business Name] now has direct online ordering. Same pizza. Cut the middleman. [Link]."
  3. Add a photo of your best-selling pizza.
  4. Add your brand colours if you have them. Otherwise keep it clean.
  5. Download and post to Instagram, Facebook, and your Google Business profile updates.
Use AI to write a better caption. Prompt: "Write a casual Instagram caption for a NZ pizza shop announcing they now have direct online ordering. Include a small offer (free drink with first order). Keep it under 60 words, no hashtag spam."

Free AI Tools Stack

Ordermeal charges you commissions partly because you feel like you can't run your own digital presence. These free tools change that. You can handle social media, customer replies, menu copy, review responses, and email marketing in under 2 hours a week.

Your Free AI Stack

  • Claude.ai (free) - Write social posts, respond to reviews, create menu descriptions, draft emails to customers. Best for anything text-based.
  • Canva (free) - Design social posts, specials graphics, and in-store signage. No design skills needed.
  • Buffer (free tier) - Schedule 3 social platforms and 10 posts ahead. Plan your month in one sitting.
  • Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts) - Build your customer email list and send weekly specials. Zero ongoing cost.
  • ChatGPT (free) - Use for brainstorming menu names, ad headlines, loyalty offer ideas, and seasonal campaign concepts.
  • Google Analytics (free) - Connect to your website to see where orders come from, which pages get traffic, and what's not working.
1
How to use Claude to respond to a bad Google review
+
  1. Go to claude.ai and create a free account
  2. Paste the review into Claude along with this prompt:
  3. "I run [Name] pizza in [Location]. A customer left this review: [paste review]. Write a professional, warm reply that acknowledges their concern, doesn't get defensive, and invites them back. Keep it under 80 words."
  4. Copy Claude's response, lightly personalise if needed, and paste it as your Google review reply.
  5. Repeat for every review. Takes 2 minutes per review.
Responding to reviews improves your Google ranking. Businesses that reply consistently get more calls and more orders. This is free and takes minutes.

Google Ads Without an Agency

Google Ads shows your pizza shop to people who are actively searching for pizza right now in your suburb. You don't need an agency. You don't need experience. A $10 to $20/day NZD budget and the right setup gets you in front of hungry people within 24 hours.
Budget recommendation: Start at $15 NZD/day. Run for 14 days. If your cost per order is under $8, increase to $30/day. If over $15, come back to this section and adjust your targeting.
1
Set up a Google Search campaign for pizza orders
+
  1. Go to ads.google.com. Create an account with your Google Business email.
  2. Click "New Campaign." Choose Goal: Sales or Website Traffic.
  3. Campaign type: Search.
  4. Set your targeting to: your suburb and surrounding 5km. Select "People in or regularly in this area."
  5. Keywords to use (exact and phrase match only): "pizza [your suburb]", "pizza delivery [your suburb]", "pizza takeaway [your city]", "best pizza near me [city]"
  6. Write 3 ads. Each needs 3 headlines and 2 descriptions. Example headline: "[Business Name] - Order Direct | No Fees, Fast Delivery | Pizza [Suburb]"
  7. Set daily budget: $15 NZD. Set to "Clicks" bidding strategy to start.
  8. Link your destination URL to your ordering page, not your homepage.
  9. Click Publish. Your ads go live within a few hours.
Stop check: Check your campaign 48 hours later. If your CTR (click-through rate) is below 2%, your headlines are too weak. Rewrite them. If CTR is above 5%, great. If you're getting clicks but no orders, your ordering page needs work.

Common Mistakes

  • Using broad match keywords (you'll pay for people searching "pizza recipes")
  • Targeting too large a radius (people 30km away won't order delivery)
  • Sending ad traffic to your homepage instead of the order page
  • Changing ad settings before they've run for 7 days (the algorithm needs data)
  • Not tracking conversions (you won't know if it's working)

Social Media on Autopilot

Social media for a pizza shop is not complicated. You don't need a strategy. You need one hour a month to create 30 days of content using Claude and Canva, then Buffer schedules it automatically. Done.
1
Create 30 days of posts in one sitting
+
  1. Open Claude.ai. Paste this prompt: "I run [Business Name], a pizza restaurant in [Location, NZ]. Write 30 social media post captions for Instagram and Facebook. Mix these: 10 product/pizza posts, 8 behind the scenes, 5 customer reviews (I'll add the real ones), 4 offers/specials, 3 local area callouts. Casual tone. Under 60 words each. No hashtag spam. No corporate speak."
  2. Copy all 30 captions into a Google Doc or Notion page.
  3. Open Canva. Create 30 matching visuals using your own pizza photos or Canva's free food templates.
  4. Open Buffer (buffer.com), connect Instagram and Facebook.
  5. Upload each post with its caption and schedule one per day across 30 days.
  6. Repeat this process once a month. Takes about 60 to 90 minutes total.
Your own photos outperform stock every time. Five minutes with your phone on a sunny day gives you better content than anything an agency would produce.

30-Day Content Mix Copy This

  • Week 1: New or seasonal special. Behind the scenes. Customer shoutout. Weekend special.
  • Week 2: Your best-seller with a story. Staff intro or kitchen shot. Direct order link post. Local area reference.
  • Week 3: "How we make our [signature pizza]" short video or photo series. Google review share. Mid-week deal.
  • Week 4: End of month offer. Loyalty reminder. A "why order direct" post. Weekend announcement.

Loyalty and Repeat Orders

The customer who orders twice a month is worth ten times more than the customer who orders once. A simple loyalty system and a small email list brings them back without any ongoing cost. Here's the simplest version that actually works.
1
Set up a simple digital loyalty system
+
  1. Option 1 (free): Stamp Me app. Download Stamp Me Business from the App Store. Create a digital stamp card. Customers get a stamp per order. 10 stamps = free pizza. They scan a QR code at checkout.
  2. Option 2 (free): Square Loyalty. If you used Square Online for ordering, Square has a built-in loyalty points system. Enable it in your Square dashboard. Customers accumulate points automatically on every order.
  3. Print a small QR code card or sticker for your counter and every bag: "Join our loyalty program. Free pizza after 10 orders. [QR code]"
  4. Collect emails via the loyalty signup. Export this list monthly to Mailchimp for your email campaigns.
Even 50 loyal customers ordering twice a month at $40 average is $4,000/month in direct, commission-free revenue. The maths makes loyalty worth prioritising immediately.
2
Send your first email campaign to 100 customers
+
  1. Collect customer emails via your ordering system (Square or Wix both export customer emails).
  2. Import to Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts). Create a simple list called "Pizza Customers."
  3. Use Claude to write your first email: "Write a short email from [Business Name] in [Location] to existing customers announcing our direct ordering. Include a one-time offer (e.g. free garlic bread on first direct order). Warm tone. Under 120 words."
  4. In Mailchimp, create a campaign. Use their free email templates. Paste your Claude-written copy. Add a big CTA button: "Order Direct Now."
  5. Send it on a Thursday or Friday evening. That's when pizza orders spike.

90-Day Action Plan

Don't try to do everything at once. Here's the week-by-week sequence that gets you from Ordermeal-dependent to fully independent without overwhelming your schedule. Three weeks of setup. Eleven weeks of growing your direct customer base.

Week 1 to 2: The Escape Critical

  • Complete Section 1 audit. Know exactly what they control.
  • Initiate domain transfer if required. Don't wait.
  • Claim and clean up your Google Business Profile.
  • Set up Square Online ordering. Test it. Go live.

Week 3 to 4: The Shift

  • Post the "order direct" announcement across all channels.
  • Put tent cards and bag stickers out in-store.
  • Set up your loyalty system (Stamp Me or Square).
  • Create first 30 days of social content with Claude and Buffer.

Week 5 to 8: The Build

  • Launch Google Ads at $15 NZD/day. Review after 7 days.
  • Build your email list. Send your first campaign.
  • Cancel Ordermeal once direct orders exceed 60% of your online volume.
  • Track commission savings weekly. Screenshot the number. This keeps you motivated.

Week 9 to 12: The Compound

  • Review Google Ads. Scale budget if cost-per-order is under $8 NZD.
  • Run your second email campaign with a seasonal offer.
  • Use AI to refresh your menu descriptions and Google profile copy.
  • Calculate total commission saved since week 1. That's your ROI.
The big picture: By week 12 you should have zero Ordermeal dependency, a growing direct customer list, an active Google Ads campaign, and a social media content system running mostly on its own. The first three weeks are the hard part. Everything after is compounding.
