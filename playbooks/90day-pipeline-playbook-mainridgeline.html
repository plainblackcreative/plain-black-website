01
Google Business Profile
The free foundation that drives local calls
Pending
Your Google Business Profile is your most important free marketing asset. HVAC is one of the highest-intent search categories on Google. People searching "AC repair Dallas" or "furnace installation near me" are ready to call. If your profile is incomplete or wrong, you are invisibly losing jobs every single day.
ℹ️
Before you start: You need a verified Google account and a physical or service-area address for Dallas. If you do not have a Google Business Profile yet, go to business.google.com and create one now. If you already have one, log in and follow along.

What to check and fix

Core info

Business name, phone number, and website must be exactly consistent with what appears on your website and any other directories. Google cross-references these. Any mismatch hurts your local ranking.

DIY Steps How to set up your service area correctly
▼
1
Go to business.google.com and select your profile. Click "Edit profile" on the left.
2
Click "Business location". If you are service-area-only, turn off the physical address toggle. Then click "Service area".
3
Add Dallas, Plano, Irving, Garland, Mesquite, and any surrounding cities you actually serve. Do not add areas you cannot reach in under 45 minutes.
4
Click Save. Google may re-verify your profile. Check your email for verification steps.
Sanity check: Search "HVAC repair Dallas" on Google Maps on your phone. If your business appears in the map pack, this is set up correctly. If it does not appear after 2 weeks with a complete profile, you may have a verification issue. Check your Profile status dashboard.

Local Service Ads (Google Guaranteed)
The highest-intent leads on the internet, before paid search
Pending
Local Service Ads appear above everything: above Google Ads, above organic results. People clicking LSAs are ready to hire. The Google Guaranteed badge is a trust signal that converts better than almost any other ad format for trades. The verification process is more involved than regular ads, but it is worth every step.
⚠️
Common mistake: People give up on LSAs because the verification takes time. Do not start this section and leave it half-done. Set aside a full afternoon to gather your documents and submit everything in one sitting.

What you need before starting

DIY Steps LSA signup and verification walkthrough
▼
1
Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads. Click "Get Started." Select HVAC as your business type.
2
Enter your business details. Make sure they match your Google Business Profile exactly. Mismatch here will cause verification delays.
3
Upload your Texas HVAC license, liability insurance certificate, and workers' comp documents. Google will review these within 1–5 business days.
4
Set your weekly budget. Start at $200/week. You pay per lead, not per click. Adjust after two weeks of data.

Set your job types to: AC repair, AC installation, furnace repair, furnace installation, heat pump services. Do not select types you cannot handle well.
Sanity check: Once verified, search "HVAC repair Dallas" in Google. Your LSA ad should appear in the top 3 green badges above all other results. If it is not appearing after 48 hours of being live, check your bid settings and service area configuration.

03
Call Handling System
Speed-to-lead is the job. Stop losing calls after hours.
Pending
The fastest responder wins. For HVAC in Dallas summers, people calling about a broken AC are not browsing options. They are calling until someone picks up. If that is not you, it is your competitor. Research consistently shows that responding within 5 minutes increases conversion rates by over 300% compared to 30 minutes.
🚨
This is your biggest leak. Most HVAC owners lose 30–50% of potential bookings to missed calls and slow callbacks. Fix this before you spend a dollar on ads. You are paying for traffic you are not capturing.

Common mistakes to fix right now

Voicemail without a callback SLA If your voicemail doesn't say "we will call you back within 30 minutes," callers assume they will wait forever and call someone else.
No after-hours coverage HVAC emergencies happen at 10pm. If you don't have a way to capture those calls, you are losing your highest-urgency, lowest-price-sensitivity jobs.
Slow quote follow-up If you send a quote and don't follow up within 2 hours, the job is probably gone. Most trades don't follow up at all.
Single phone point of failure One mobile number, no backup. One busy signal costs you a job. Use a virtual number with call forwarding.

05
Meta Ads: Awareness and Booking
Reach Dallas homeowners before they need you
In Progress
Meta ads for HVAC work differently from Google ads. Google captures people actively searching. Meta reaches Dallas homeowners before they have a problem, so when their AC fails at 11pm in August, Ridge Line is the name they already recognize. This is brand + demand, not just intent capture.
ℹ️
Meta home services benchmark: average CPC around $2.18, average cost per lead around $24. At $500/month spend, you should be generating 15–20+ leads. If your cost per lead is above $40, something is wrong with your targeting or creative.

Campaign structure for Ridge Line

Campaign 1: Awareness (always-on)

Objective: Reach. Creative: before/after installs, team shots, "Dallas summers are brutal" angle. Target: Dallas homeowners 30–65, homeownership interest, home improvement. Budget: $10/day.

Campaign 2: Lead generation (season-sensitive)

Objective: Leads (Meta lead form). Creative: seasonal angle, "Is your AC ready for summer?" or "Don't get caught with a broken furnace." Offer: free tune-up inspection quote, or "$50 off your next service." Budget: $20/day May through September, $10/day rest of year.

DIY Steps Setting up your first Ridge Line Meta campaign
▼
1
Go to business.facebook.com. Make sure you have a Facebook Business Page and an Ads Manager account. If not, create both first.
2
Click "Create" in Ads Manager. Select objective: Leads.
3
Ad Set targeting: Location = Dallas, TX + 25 mile radius. Age = 28–65. Interests = Home improvement, Home ownership, HVAC. Keep this broad at first.
4
Set budget: $20/day. Do not set a lifetime budget. Daily budget gives you more control while you're testing.
5
Create your lead form. Keep it to 3 fields maximum: Name, Phone number, Service needed. Every extra field loses you leads.
6
Do NOT touch the campaign for 7 days after launch. Meta's algorithm needs data to optimize. Changing it early resets the learning phase.
Sanity check at day 7: Cost per lead should be under $35. CTR should be above 1%. If cost per lead is above $50 at day 7, change your creative (not your targeting). If CTR is below 0.5%, your headline or image is the problem.

Your 90-Day Action Schedule
Week by week. What to do and when to check it.
Pending
This is the schedule for Ridge Line HVAC. Not a general framework. Your specific trade, your market, your budget. Follow it in order. Do not skip to week 6 because you feel impatient.
Week 1–2 · Foundation

Complete Google Business Profile. Submit LSA verification documents. Set up your virtual phone number and missed-call text-back. Install Meta Pixel on your website (see Section 4). Start Day 1 Meta awareness campaign at $10/day using interest-based targeting. Do not touch any campaigns after launch.

Week 3–4 · Capture

LSA should be verified by now. Turn on LSA ads at $200/week budget. Launch Meta lead campaign at $20/day. Check Google Business insights: how many calls, direction requests, and website visits. If LSA is not verified yet, follow up with Google support chat.

Week 5–8 · Optimize

Review Meta campaigns. Keep the top-performing ad, pause the others. Check your Meta cost per lead: target under $30. Launch Google Search Ads (see Section 6) if LSA alone is not generating enough volume. Send review request messages to all recent completed jobs.

Week 9–12 · Scale

If cost per lead is under $35 and you are closing jobs, increase Meta daily budget by 20% (not more). Start retargeting campaign for website visitors (see Section 9). Review your 90-day numbers: how many leads, how many jobs, what is your cost per booked job. Adjust budget allocation accordingly.

⚠️
The most common failure point: People complete setup and then do not check their numbers for 6 weeks. Set a recurring 30-minute calendar block every Monday to review: leads in, calls answered, jobs booked. One number to track: cost per booked job. Everything else is noise.
