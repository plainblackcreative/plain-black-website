Redesign Rationale
What was broken · What we fixed · Why it matters
01
No Visual Hierarchy
The current site dumps images and text in a single column with no focal point. Visitors have no idea where to look first.
The new design uses a split hero with a clear headline, supporting copy, and a single primary CTA. Eyes land in the right place within 3 seconds.
02
Zero Brand Identity
White background, stock WordPress fonts, no colour palette. Nothing communicates "premium Papamoa dining."
A deep berry and gold palette with Playfair Display serif type. Warm, premium, and distinctly hospitality. The brand now feels worth the price point.
03
Buried Conversion Points
The 20% online discount is hidden at the bottom of a wall of text. The reservation form is hard to find. Functions are barely mentioned.
CTA buttons appear in the hero, nav bar, info strip, and order banner. The 20% offer is front and centre. Reservations live in the nav at all times.
04
Not Mobile-Ready
The current site is technically responsive but collapses on mobile — tiny text, no real tap targets, images that don't crop sensibly.
Mobile layout is purpose-built: large tap targets, full-width hero, scrollable info bar, card-based menu grid, and a prominent order CTA with coupon code.
05
Spelling and Copy Issues
The current site contains typos and incomplete sentences ("private function which We can cater") that undermine trust with first-time visitors.
All copy is professionally rewritten. Concise, confident, and consistent in tone. The restaurant reads like what it actually is: a quality local eatery.
06
Key Info Buried at the Bottom
Opening hours, phone number, and address all require scrolling. Most visitors arrive wanting to know if you're open and where you are.
A persistent info bar directly below the hero shows hours, location, phone, and function capacity — the four things 80% of visitors need first.
What You're Selling Them

A complete brand and web presence upgrade: a new colour system, new typography, rebuilt layout optimised for both desktop and mobile, rewritten copy throughout, and a conversion-focused structure that turns casual browsers into diners and enquiries. The current site is costing them bookings every single day — this is the fix.
