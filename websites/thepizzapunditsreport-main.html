Critical Issues

What needs fixing today

These are not minor tweaks — they are active problems costing Manoj customers and revenue right now.

!
Urgent

TripAdvisor links to a competitor's website and email

The Pizza Pundits' TripAdvisor profile still carries the website URL and email address of The Pizza Library — the previous owner, who operates a separate restaurant at 314 Maunganui Road, Mt Maunganui. Every customer who clicks through from TripAdvisor is being sent directly to a competitor. This is the most urgent fix in this entire report and can be resolved in under 10 minutes.

!
Urgent

TripAdvisor listing still carries the old brand name

The listing is still displayed under "The Pizza Library Papamoa." Owner responses are also signed as "Pizza Library." Customers searching for The Pizza Pundits will not find a matching result — and existing customers who want to recommend the new brand cannot do so clearly. The TripAdvisor listing needs to be formally claimed and updated.

!
Urgent

No Google Business Profile

No verified Google Business Profile was found for The Pizza Pundits. This means no Google Maps pin under the new name, no photo gallery, no menu, no posts, no Q&A, and no ability to appear in "pizza near me" searches. Google Business is free and is the single highest-ROI digital action a local business can take.

2
Important

No rebrand announcement anywhere online

The transition from Pizza Library Papamoa to The Pizza Pundits has not been communicated on any platform. Long-time customers may not know the business is under new ownership. New customers searching "Pizza Library Papamoa" encounter inconsistent information. A single well-crafted post across Facebook, Instagram, and Google Business would resolve this immediately.

3
Important

No owned website — all revenue flows through third-party platforms

Every order placed via Uber Eats, DoorDash, Delivereasy, or OrderMeal costs the business an estimated 25–35% in commission fees. There is no way to capture customer emails, run a loyalty programme, or rank on Google without an owned web presence. A basic one-page site with a direct ordering link would reduce platform dependency and build long-term customer data ownership.

What's working

Real strengths to build on

The product and community reputation are genuinely strong. The digital layer just needs to catch up.

✓
Strength

4.9-star TripAdvisor rating with 58 reviews

This is an exceptional quality signal. Reviewers specifically call out the quirky atmosphere, handmade gluten-free bases, unique pizza names (including "The Narnia"), and warm service. These are memorable differentiators that most chains can't replicate.

✓
Strength

Homemade gluten-free bases from raw ingredients

Multiple reviews specifically call this out as rare and impressive. This is a niche marketing angle that appeals to a large and passionate dietary community — and it's almost never mentioned in the business's own marketing copy.

✓
Strength

Active community involvement — youth sports sponsorship

Reviews reference youth sports team vouchers. This kind of community investment is marketing gold — parents share everything, and local loyalty built this way is near impossible to buy. It's currently invisible online.

✓
Strength

Four active delivery platforms with strong customer feedback

Uber Eats, DoorDash, Delivereasy, and OrderMeal are all live. DoorDash has received reviews calling the quality "amazing" and saying customers won't return to major chains. This customer sentiment is a conversion asset waiting to be amplified.

Review Volume

58 TripAdvisor reviews vs. 356 for the nearest Pizza Library competitor

The Pizza Pundits has a higher average rating (4.9) than The Pizza Library Co. in Mt Maunganui (4.2) — but a fraction of the review volume. Review count directly affects local search ranking. A simple QR code on every pizza box linking to Google Reviews, combined with a staff prompt at pickup, could add 100+ reviews within 90 days at zero cost.

Social Content

The product is visually compelling — but there are almost no photos online

Handcrafted gourmet pizza with quirky names, a distinctive interior, and visible community values is exactly the kind of content that performs on Instagram and TikTok. A consistent 3-posts-per-week rhythm — pizza of the week, behind-the-scenes dough work, and community stories — would build a local following within months.
