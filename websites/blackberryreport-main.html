Section 01
SEO — Search Engine Optimisation
Score: 28 / 100
Critical
Page Title is Generic and Wasted
The current page title is "HOME - Blackberry Eatery". This is one of the most valuable pieces of real estate in SEO and it's being thrown away. Google shows this in search results and uses it to understand what the page is about.
Fix: Change to something like "Blackberry Eatery | Café, Brunch & Dinner in Papamoa Beach" — lead with the brand, follow with services and location. Every page should have a unique, keyword-rich title under 60 characters.
Critical
No Meta Description Present
There is no meta description tag on the homepage. Google will auto-generate one from random page content — which is almost always suboptimal and loses the chance to control first impressions in search results.
Fix: Write a 150–160 character meta description for every page. Example: "Papamoa Beach's favourite café for breakfast, brunch and dinner. Allpress coffee, locally sourced kai, cocktails and functions for up to 120 guests. Book online."
Critical
No H1 Tag — Heading Structure is Broken
The page has no H1 heading, which is the primary signal Google uses to understand what a page is about. The site uses H2 tags for contact details ("Fashion Island, Shop 1/42 Gravatt Road") which is structurally backwards and confusing for search engines.
Fix: Add one clear H1 to the homepage such as "Café, Brunch & Dinner in Papamoa Beach." Demote address and contact info to paragraph tags or a footer element. Use H2s for actual section headings like "Our Menu" and "Functions."
Critical
All Images Missing Alt Text
Every image on the site (at least 10 visible on the homepage alone) has empty or missing alt attributes. Alt text helps Google understand image content, contributes to image search rankings, and is required for accessibility compliance.
Fix: Add descriptive alt text to all images. Examples: "Blackberry Eatery interior Papamoa Beach" / "Lamb shank dinner dish Blackberry Eatery" / "Barista preparing coffee at Blackberry Eatery Papamoa."
Critical
No Structured Data (Schema Markup)
The site has zero schema markup. For a restaurant, LocalBusiness and Restaurant schema are essential — they tell Google exactly what type of business this is, where it is, when it opens, and what it serves. This directly affects how results display in Google Maps, search cards, and AI overviews.
Fix: Add Restaurant schema JSON-LD to the site's <head>. Include: name, address, phone, openingHours, servesCuisine, priceRange, menu URL, aggregateRating, and hasMap. This can be added via a WordPress plugin or manually in the theme.
Warning
Reservation Page URL is a SEO Disaster
The reservations page URL is: /contact-form-7-iddf65412-titlereservation/ — this is auto-generated plugin garbage. It provides zero keyword value and looks unprofessional in search results and when shared.
Fix: Change the slug to /reservations/ or /book-a-table/ and set up a 301 redirect from the old URL.
Warning
Menu Page is Labelled "Dinner Menu" but Serves Breakfast & Lunch
The menu page URL is /dinner-menu/ but the nav link reads "Breakfast & Lunch Menu." This inconsistency confuses both search engines and users, and is likely losing searches for "Blackberry Eatery breakfast menu Papamoa."
Fix: Consolidate to a single /menu/ URL covering all meal periods, or create separate pages: /breakfast-menu/ and /dinner-menu/ with proper titles and descriptions.
Warning
Body Copy Contains Spelling and Grammar Errors
The functions section reads: "private function which We can cater for up to 120 people... also we do outdoor carering." These errors signal low content quality to Google's helpful content system and undermine trust with potential customers reading the page.
Fix: Rewrite all body copy. Prioritise the functions section and about section. Use natural language that incorporates local keywords: "Papamoa Beach," "Bay of Plenty," "Allpress coffee," "brunch Papamoa," "licensed restaurant."
Pass
SSL Certificate Active
The site is served over HTTPS, which is a baseline Google ranking signal. This is in order.
Pass
Site is Being Indexed by Google
The site appears in Google search results, meaning it is being crawled and indexed. The homepage was last seen indexed in October 2025. The site is discoverable — it just isn't being found for the right terms.

Section 02
GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation
Score: 44 / 100
GEO measures how likely an AI model (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Siri) is to mention or recommend Blackberry Eatery when someone asks "where should I eat in Papamoa?" The key inputs are: third-party mentions, review volume, structured data, and clear factual content on the website itself.
Pass
Strong Third-Party Presence
Blackberry Eatery appears on TripAdvisor (ranked #6–9 of 44 restaurants in Papamoa, 4.5 stars, 37–39 reviews), Google Maps (4.4 stars), Facebook (2,100 followers), Restaurant Guru, Sluurpy, and Find Me Gluten Free. This multi-platform footprint is exactly what AI models pull from when generating local recommendations. This is the strongest area of their digital presence.
Warning
Review Volume is Too Low for Competitive AI Mentions
With 37–39 TripAdvisor reviews and an estimated 100–200 Google reviews, Blackberry is competing against venues like Thai Thani (167 reviews) and Rain Bar (229 reviews). AI models weight review volume heavily when deciding who to recommend. The ratings are excellent — there just aren't enough of them.
Fix: Launch an active review generation campaign. Train staff to ask for Google reviews at checkout. Add a QR code table card linking directly to the Google review page. Target 50 new Google reviews in 3 months.
Critical
NAP Data is Inconsistent Across Platforms
Name, Address, and Phone (NAP) data varies across listings. The website lists two phone numbers (02041516171 and 075721724). Third-party directories show different numbers and slightly different address formats. AI models and Google treat inconsistent NAP as a trust signal failure.
Fix: Standardise NAP across all platforms to one format: "Blackberry Eatery, Shop 1/42 Gravatt Road, Papamoa Beach 3118. Phone: 07 572 1724." Update the website, Google Business Profile, Facebook, TripAdvisor, and any other listings to match exactly.
Critical
Google Business Profile Likely Incomplete
Based on search results, the Google Business Profile appears to be missing: a complete menu upload, post activity, Q&A responses, updated photos from 2024–2026, and service category tags (Functions, Catering, Online Ordering). An incomplete GBP significantly reduces the chance of appearing in AI Overviews for local searches.
Fix: Audit and complete the full GBP. Add all service categories, upload the current menu as a PDF, add 10+ high-quality photos, set up weekly posts, and respond to all existing reviews (positive and negative).
Warning
No Backlink Profile Worth Noting
The site has few to no inbound links from local media, Bay of Plenty tourism sites, or food blogs. AI models and Google both use backlinks as authority signals. The site appears self-contained with no external sources pointing to it.
Fix: Get listed on Tauranga City Council's visitor site, Bay of Plenty tourism directories, and local event listings. Reach out to Bay of Plenty food bloggers for a feature. Every legitimate backlink increases authority with both Google and generative AI systems.

Section 03
AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation
Score: 18 / 100
AEO measures how well the website answers specific questions a potential customer might ask Siri, Google Assistant, or ChatGPT — such as "Is Blackberry Eatery open on Sundays?", "Does Blackberry Eatery take bookings?", or "What food does Blackberry Eatery serve?" If the site can't answer these directly, AI tools pull from third-party sources instead — reducing control over what's said about the business.
Critical
No FAQ Content Anywhere on the Site
FAQ pages are the single most powerful AEO tool available to a local business. Questions like "Do you cater for dietary requirements?", "Can I book a private function?", "Do you have outdoor seating?", "Is Blackberry Eatery licensed?" are being asked — by humans and AI systems — and the site answers none of them directly.
Fix: Add a FAQ section to the homepage and a dedicated FAQ page. Structure each question as an H3 tag with a direct answer in the paragraph below. Use FAQPage schema markup so Google can display answers directly in search results.
Critical
Opening Hours Not Marked Up for Machines
Opening hours appear on the page as styled italic text inside a visual layout. They are not in a machine-readable format. When someone asks Google Assistant or Siri "Is Blackberry Eatery open right now?", the system cannot reliably extract the answer from the website — it has to rely on the Google Business Profile instead.
Fix: Add OpeningHoursSpecification to the Restaurant schema markup. Also ensure GBP hours are always current and match the website exactly. Discrepancies between the two are flagged as trust issues by Google.
Critical
No Clear "What We Serve" Summary for AI Extraction
The about copy is florid but vague ("a culinary gem," "a gastronomic adventure"). AI models struggle to extract specific, factual answers from this. When asked "what kind of food does Blackberry Eatery serve," an AI would have to guess rather than state confidently: "Blackberry Eatery serves breakfast, brunch and dinner including Allpress coffee, handmade dumplings, pasta, lamb shank, cocktails and wine."
Fix: Add a concise, factual "About" block using plain language that directly states: cuisine type, meal periods, dietary options, coffee supplier, licensing status, and function capacity. This becomes the source AI models quote from.
Warning
Menu Content Not Accessible to Search Engines
The menu is only available as a PDF file hosted at a non-descriptive URL. PDFs are harder for search engines and AI models to read than HTML pages. Dish names, prices, and descriptions are invisible to most crawlers — which means the site can't appear for searches like "lamb shank dinner Papamoa" even though they serve one.
Fix: Build an HTML menu page with actual text content. Use MenuItem schema markup for dishes. This alone could generate significant long-tail organic traffic from people searching for specific dishes in the Papamoa / Tauranga area.
Warning
No Online Booking Confirmation or Visibility
The reservation form exists but there is no clear text on the site that says "We accept online bookings" in a way search engines can confirm. When AI systems are asked "Can I book a table at Blackberry Eatery online?", they cannot confidently confirm this from the website alone.
Fix: Add acceptsReservations: true to the Restaurant schema. Add explicit copy: "Book a table online — we confirm within 24 hours" with a clear link to the reservations page. Also consider integrating a proper booking system (OpenTable, Eat.nz, or SevenRooms) for real-time availability.

Summary

Blackberry Eatery has genuinely strong bones — good ratings, solid social presence, a great product, and real customer love. The problem is almost entirely digital infrastructure. The website is functionally invisible to search engines and AI systems for everything except branded searches. Someone already looking for "Blackberry Eatery" will find it. Someone looking for "best brunch Papamoa" or asking ChatGPT where to eat in the Bay of Plenty will not.

The good news: most of these fixes are technical, not creative. They don't require a brand rethink — they require implementation. A rebuilt site with proper schema, clean copy, and an HTML menu would move this business from a score of 30 to above 70 within 90 days of launch.
